Hi folks!
I just wanted to let you know that any registrations received for the
conference by midnight (your time) on Friday, May 2, will receive a fun free gift at
check-in at the conference. You can either pay by Paypal or by check, but I
need to receive your form by then.

Also, please make sure that if you want to attend the Whale Watch on Sunday
afternoon of the conference, that you register as soon as possible. The boat
can hold 150 people, including infants. I've reserved 100 spots, so far (I can
increase that to the full 150 if I need to), but we've already sold 53 spots!
If you want to go, please register very soon.

We already have LOTS of registrations, from people all over the country. This
is going to be a great conference, and I hope to meet you all there! Please
feel free to forward this to anyone you think will be interested.

Kathryn Baptista, Conference Coordinator

Come to the Live and Learn Unschooling Conference August 27-29 in Peabody, MA!
For more information, go to www.LiveandLearnConference.org

Oh how I wish I could come - but it is a bit far from New Zealand!

I just wanted to say that my kids and I went on a whale watch 2 months
ago in Kaikura, New Zealand, and it was the most awesome thing I have
ever done - I'm an atheist, but the only way I can express how I felt is
to say it was a truly spiritual experience.

If you are hesitating because of the cost quoted on the website - "Weve
arranged a special deal of $16 per person. Last season regular price
tickets were $30 for adults and $18 for children under 16, but theyre
planning to go up this Spring." - note well - we paid $NZ110 each for me
and my 16yo, and $60 for the 13yo. That $NZ280 equates to about $US187,
as opposed to the $US78 it would cost us if we did it at the conference.

I would pay twice what I did here, and still feel it was worth it.

Cally
