I've also heard of Singin' Smart tapes, which used to be available through Learning Home, $12, phone 410-536-5990. Item TPZ001 - government, US, europe, planets, composers, bones, myths, etiquette, seasons. Item TPZ002 - body systems, muscles, central/south american countries, africa, continents and seas, major mountains, preamble to constitution, famous writers, inventors, explorers, fun with puns, dewey decimals, and 41 common elements.

Hi Ann-
I'm a lurker on this list, but you wrote about your
son being an auditory learner and wondered about
tapes, etc.

My 11yob is an auditory learner, too and I get every
kind of tape I can think of. He loves books on tape
from the library...even ones that might seem boring to
others. He loves to listen to the radio. We have a
public radio station that plays old time radio plays
on Sunday evenings and he just loves those.
I also read aloud to him just as much as I possibly
can and I'm always amazed at how much he remembers and
knows about the stories and characters.
I bought him his own CD Walkman for Christmas and he
just loves to wear his earphones and listen to books
on CD. He goes to sleep that way, in fact.
I would just encourage you to find ways to aid your
son in this style, because helping my son find
auditory ways to learn has just really helped him.
He's very non-verbal and has a hard time writing, so
he had a tendency to feel insecure in groups where
those skills are valued (namely, school!) But,
showing him that there is a way that he can learn
easily has really encouraged him.
Boomerang is great, I have to say. We're lucky to
have it at our library. Maybe you could suggest the
purchase to the library? I know our system is open to
suggestions from readers. Or maybe somebody out there
could buy it for him for a birthday/Christmas gift?
My daughter wanted it so badly that she saved up money
from babysitting, etc. to buy it for herself, so now
we don't even check it out from the library!
Keep looking...sound like you're on the right track!
Carol B.
