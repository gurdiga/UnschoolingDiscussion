When we received a new box of checks in the mail today, I was reminded of
something that happened a few years ago with my eldest, Colton. We'd gotten
a batch of checks from the bank that had been incorrectly imprinted. We were
going to throw the checks away, but Colton asked if he could have a couple
packs and if I would show him how to fill them out. I did, and for the next
few days I received checks from Colton written as payment for everything from
macaroni and cheese dinners to clean laundry.

One morning I woke up very early, long before anyone else was up. I came
downstairs, made some tea, and settled on the sofa with a blanket to read.
When I opened my book, a check fluttered to the floor. It was made out to
me, in the amount of $22.00. Written in the bottom left corner in the memo
section were two miraculous words: For love.

I still have the check and I will keep it forever. But it's worth a lot
more to me than $22.00. It's priceless.

Carol
