the upcoming Live and Learn Conference, in St. Louis, MO.

change, what we learned, .....

Deanne Brown

2 Oct 2005, at 1:51pm

Hi Pam, I met you at the HSC conference in Sacramento this year and I
was in the audience at your Radical Unschooling Talk. I am a friend of
Cindi's from SOCHS. I was completely moved and changed by your talk.
Your explanation of what unschooling "is" and what it "isn't" clarified
so much for me. Most importantly it made me realize that we are living
an unschooled life. We had reached our goal and now we can get on with
living. This was a huge moment for me as I sat listening to you with
tears in my eyes.

If you were to talk about some of the same things you did at that
conference and simply add Roya's point of view, I suspect that would
answer a lot of questions that people may have about your life as
unschoolers and your relationship with each other. For example, does
Roya remember your attempt at unit studies and what did she think about
it? Also, new unschoolers always love to hear about the personal
journey towards unschooling and the moment that you took that "leap of
faith". Does Roya remember this journey and what did it look like to
her? Or was she too young?

I have three children, my son Riley is 12, my son Casey is 10 and my
daughter Maggie is 5. This is the first time I have posted. I have
been on this list for a little under a year now and have learned so
much. I think that it is a great idea to hear from Roya so she can
share her perspective. I love to hear the parent's story, but it would
be fascinating to hear what your children have to say.

Deanne
