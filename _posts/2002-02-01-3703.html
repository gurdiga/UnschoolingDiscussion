No. This is me. Deborah A Cunefare. Who'd make that up? :-)

I think that the whole idea of hiding behind psuedonyms is, well, stupid. In
the case of kids, it takes the focus off the REAL dangers and gives a false
sense of security. Kids aren't in danger of being stolen away by some
stranger who learns their name and where they live and sneaks up behind them
one day as they walk innocently along. Kids are in danger from predators who
slyly work themselves into their confidence and lure them away while
encouraging them to hide from and lie to their parents. Doesn't insisting our
kids lie to others about themselves set a poor precedent?

As for adults, I think most of the nastiness to be found online is being
perpetrated by people hiding behind anonymity. I don't want to be mistaken
for one of them. And I don't believe that having people know who I am and
where I live is inherently dangerous.

Deborah in IL
