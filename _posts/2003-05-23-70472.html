I've been meaning to pass this on and perhaps it's expired, but perhaps it's
not!

We've been doing these "mystery shops" for Pulseback for 6 months or so. I
guess corporations hire Pulseback to pay local customers to visit various
stores and then report back how the experience went and whether the stores
are maintaining corporate standards. (There are other stores beside Papa
Ginos, not all restaurants. There's a list at the Pulseback website.)

Anyway, for the restaurant shops, you get paid ($15 or so depending on
whether it's lunch or dinner) to order something and then fill out a form
(takes about 15 mintues) on the appearance of the store, courtesy of the
employees, quality of the food and so forth. Basically it's a free lunch,
depending on how many you feed. You'll have to order 1 of 2 specific items
and then the rest you order is up to you. Or nothing more if you don't want
to.

This one's for a delivery to introduce people to the idea of mystery
shopping so the form is probably a lot simpler than in store shops.

It's an interesting thing to do, free lunch and they only ask every couple
of months or so. There's no requirement to do any of the shops they send
emails out for. It's all voluntary.

Joyce
