Thank you for this, Tania:  

-=- it was the reading of Sandras Big Book (after of 
course reading for years her webpage) that really clicked with me. No 
idea what the difference is for me between the web page and the book. 
And I certainly still use the webpage, but eye-opening, life-changing 
was for me the book. -=-

When I first started on that book, I expected to quote the best part of a page on my site, and link to it.  But many pages were too long, or were exchanges, or were the writings of other people, and there were way more pages than I could represent in one book.   So more than half of that book was new writing, and some of it was that sort of writing where I got in the flow and great summaries and new ideas were coming out.  There are many things there that I read and think "wow."  Some of it was really nice.  

Some of it has been plagiarized, too, and it's harder to find that because it's not online and so searchable, so that's flattering and irritating. :-)

I do think it's a good resource, and a good set of short reads, and maybe for some a good solid-through read.  For anyone here who isn't aware of it, in Europe it's best bought through Amazon, and in the U.S. Amazon if you have Amazon prime.  U.S. without amazon prime, or others who can't get it from Amazon:  http://sandradodd.com/bigbook/

Or (there are links in there) e-copy through lulu.

With Lulu, if you're getting an eBook, Moving a Puddle has color photos (and lots of them), because back then I uploaded it in color which was "wrong," and awkward. :-)  So for the Big Book, I made a black and white file, and sent that "right."  Then electronic versions came along and wrong became a big advantage!

http://sandradodd.com/puddlebook

Sandra
