I know there's a new book called "Christian Unschooling" - Don't know
the author
"Learning All the Time" by John Holt is my all-time favorite.
Alfie Kohn's "Punished by Rewards" is great.
"Summerhill" by A. S. Neill is very good.
"Dumbing Us Down" by John Taylor Gatto is fabulous
Read all the "Growing Without Schooling" magazines you can get your
hands on.
"Child's Work" was very good. Don't remember the author.
"I Learn Better By Teaching Myself" Don't remember author
"The Hurried Child" by David Elkind

Not all of these focus exclusively on unschooling, but I found them all
very interesting and helpful. You might want to check out John Holt's
bookstore also. Do a search of Growing Without Schooling. I was very
saddened to hear about the discontinuation of their publication. Of all
the newspapers, periodicals, magazines, etc. we receive at this house
(and, there are lots!!) Growing Without Schooling was the only one I
would immediately sit down and read, cover to cover, as soon as it came
to my mailbox. We have lost a wonderful resource.

Mimi

Anyone who has read Summerhill might want to try a fairly recent book by
Matthew Appleton, a former houseparent at the school (yes, its a school,
but a very unusual one). Its called A Free Range Childhood. Id even
recommend it over Neills books (which have become very dated in some ways),
as an introduction to them. Matthew (I know him because I was one of the
interpreters for some talks he gave in Madrid years ago) writes very clearly
about what its really like to work and live with children growing up in
freedom.

The author of Childs Work is Nancy Wallace. Her first book was Better Than
School - also excellent.

I think Leistico is the author of I Learn Better By Teaching Myself.

The Holt Associates website is at http://www.holtgws.com
<http://www.holtgws.com/>

You can link from the website to FUN Books, which bought out the book end of
Holt Associates. You can still buy back issues of GWS, and some other
things, on the Holt website itself, and they still do consulting and give
talks.

These (along with the Home Education Magazine catalog) are good places to
buy from, rather than from a big chain, if you want to help support people
connected to unschooling.

Elsa Haas

Hi Cathy,

I'm in the USA, also have 5 children and consider myself a Christian
unschooler. I think the ease of reconciling the two depends on your view of
Christianity and God. If you see people as basically evil who need to be
coerced with the threat of hell and damnation to be "good", then unschooling
can be more difficult to accept.

Personally, I remember how Jesus said we should be like children, how he
honored them, how he himself taught. He lived his life, he talked and asked
questions. He never forced people to listen and take note.

I was at a support group meeting and the topic was "Scheduling our
homeschool days--a guide to lesson planning". I thought "oh yuck", but
wanted to go see some of my friends I knew would be there. The speaker told
about how she prays for God to show her what her children need to know, what
to teach them. I thought about that and figured my kids don't need me as a
go between for them and God. I think if he is looking out for me, he is
looking out for them and if he needs them to know something, he will let
them know.

Julie
