Smacking kids is not biblical and she does NOT have a point.

Spare the Rod and Spoil the Child (which I am assuming is the biblical
reference at issue here) speaks about GUIDING those for whom one is
responsible, not smacking 'em.

The Rod spoken of, is the shepherds rod, their crook (think Mary Go Peep
with her sheep sort-of-a-thing.) Remember, the bible is set in a
shepherding-rich society! Their sheep are their livelihood! No sheep, no
riches and no food for the family! Shepherds used their rods to GENTLY
nudge the sheep on the side to keep them on the path to the fields/market,
to lever them out from gullies, to defend the flock against predators,
etc. Spare the rod and not only don't have any sheep at market, but you've
got a whole bunch of them stuck in the bottom of gullies and eaten by
sharp-toothed critters!

So, the next time someone uses the rod reference as a defense for smackin'
kids, you'll be able to guide them into a more Christ-like pathway.

It's Spare the Guidance and Spoil the Child, not Spare the Smackin' and
Spoil the Child!

decided she was Athiest after all. I can still speak the lingo when called
upon, though.
