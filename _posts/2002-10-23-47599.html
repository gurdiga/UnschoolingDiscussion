Here is the problem I encounter when considering whether something is
worthy of being reported. I know from experience (working for social
services) that the conditions in foster homes is not always good,
sometime even horrific. I also know, because I worked with them, that
social workers are as prone to bias and rashness as anyone else. They
cannot always be depended on to use calm reason in making a decision
about what action to take.

One doesn't have to work in the field to know how traumatic being removed
from the home can be for a child. I always wonder if I would be causing
them to be taken from one situation to something worse.

As much as I don't like spanking I think most children are better off in
this situation than being taken away and put somewhere that could be a
much worse danger. Even if the foster home is a good one it's not worth
the trauma of being taken, not when it comes to spanking. I guess as
long as flawed human beings are raising children we will have less than
perfect answers to the problems.

Kris
