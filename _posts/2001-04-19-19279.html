I'm assuming you use Windows. Go to "Start" then "Find" and have it look
for these files,
kern32.exe
inetd.exe
hkk32.exe *note* this file means that the "trojan horse" aspect of the
virus was executed and it is the cause for real concern. It can capture
and transmit passwords, credit card information and other security
information stored on your hard drive.

If you don't have any of these files you are probably fine. If you
haven't opened any attachments for the past couple of weeks you are fine.
If you have opened ANY attachment from an email with no message except a
quote from one of your own posts along with a note "look to the
attachment" then you have the virus.

Johanna - Do not feel bad about the lack of anti-virus software.  I have the software, yet I was affected. Everybody who uses the internet/e-mail knows they are open to this risk.  Thankfully, the software is available online.  No need to apologize (more on that later), although your apology is graciously accepted by all, no doubt.  By the way,  I have an extra McAfee program (got it free as an incentive for something else - e-mail me if you want me to mail it).  When I had the full suite on my older computer, it left too much running and slowed things down.  If this is a concern for you, have a self-proclaimed "expert" (the kind that do things for free) help you install it differently.  It has more than just anti-virus software on it.  Also, you'll need to update it often.  The Symantec I have know updates ever 14 days with an option to do it sooner. 
 
Back to the original thought here - no one blames you anyway (especially me).  Truth be known, I suspect that I got it from a very small, twelve woman list from a person who didn't know she had it.  By the time I figured out I had it, it had sent dozens, maybe hundreds of e-mails out - mostly to myself!  And I seem to have sent it to the 15,000+ Flylady list as well as the unschooling list.  If anyone has a red face here it's me.  So to all of you, I apologize.  What's strange is that I got rid of it days ago, and am surprised it just hit this list recently - I thought it probably hadn't.   I guess these worms are written so cleverly that the pause is intentional.  Since this was my first ever virus/worm, I am now well-schooled in the dangers of strangely and alluringly titled attachments.  However, I never received the enticement of nude photos. I've been LOL just thinking of those of you sitting out their staring at those attachment titles in bewilderment!!!
 
Please accept my humblest apologies,  Annette
