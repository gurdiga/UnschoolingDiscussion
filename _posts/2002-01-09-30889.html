> Many public schools participate in a program "Flat Stanley." It is
> based on Jeff Brown's book "Flat Stanley." In the book, Stanley
> is "flattened" by a falling bulletin board. One of the many
> advantages to being flat, is Stanley is now able to visit his friends
> by traveling in an envelope.
>
> We wanted to do something similar for our Homeschooling Families!
> Our Flat Travelers project is very simple. Your family or homeschool
> makes a paper Flat Traveler and mails it, along with a blank journal,
> to someone on the "List of Participants" or to a celebrity or
> politician. The recipient treats the visiting Traveler as a guest
> and takes it places. After a week your Traveler is mailed back with
> a completed journal and perhaps some photographs, post cards, and
> souvenirs. You read about what your Flat Traveler has done, where
> he/she has been and plot his/her travels on a map.
>
> Not only is it a great way to learn geography, but it's really FUN
> too! What we need right now is for you to join the group. We're
> just starting out, and we need families from around the world who
> would like to participate. Please feel free to forward this message
> to anyone you think might be interested.
>
> To learn more about the FlatTravelers group, please visit
> http://groups.yahoo.com/group/FlatTravelers
>
> Please email me with any questions. Hope to see you onlist!
