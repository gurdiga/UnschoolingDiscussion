Bridget,

If you are concerned about Wyndam's television watching because of how it
might affect him at some future date (not getting up for work, not paying
bills), this again isn't unschooling.
Unschooling deals with the here and now. How does watching too much
television (whatever that is) affect Wyndam as a 9yo boy today? What is he
getting out of it today? What else might interest him today? What is he
avoiding today?

The speculative long term problems were sort of in answer to someone
else's question of would their TV reception get fuzzy if they watch too
much too. As for Wyndham, left to his own obsessive nature, he would
watch TV and eat until he was an obese lump on the couch. When we tell
him to shut it off and go find something else to do, there is usually not
a struggle over it (if there is, we talk about it and forge a compromise
because he usually just really wants to see something and is waiting for
it to come on. We just tell him we'll tell him when it's time in that
case and he is happy. Then we do tell him.) But when he does turn it
off, he does go find something else to do. BUT if we don't say it, he
would never shut it off.
