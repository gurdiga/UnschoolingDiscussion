I think the problem is that you think by having set guidelines for the
family it must mean oneof us is controlling the rest. It doesn't. The
guidelines are for the benefit of all and so that each person knows what
is expected of him/her. They were workedout together by all of us in
response to a problem. That problem was my husband who could not leave
the dishes or laundry undone. Now, ANYONE who is not pulling their
weight, Ron and I included, looses the help and assistence and perks of
being an equal until they are back up to speed. A lot of this happens
unspoken, unannounced and without it being a control issue. Obviously,
this whole list system is not set in stone. Everything is variable from
week to week but it has really helped us to all be working together
effortlessly, instead of being in each otehrs way and all trying to do
the same things at once.

You all keep talking about giving up control. I didn't 'give up' control
and I am not holding on to control. I spread it around. We are all in
control now. And we are all much better friends for it.

whenever as long as they are active participant in the family. If I were

of the Flies. I know they don't. I know that you have led and cojoled
