Dear Helen,

I must tell you that from my point of view, about 50% of the posts that get
me thinking, are the ones from Ned. I frequently disagree with him, but since
I've always enjoyed a good debate, that's O.K. Although I enjoy many of the
parenting type posts, I don't have much to say on that subject. My children
are 13 and 11, and after years of playgroup and parent discussions it all is
getting a little old. A few years ago I realized that I missed the more
adult discussions I could engage in before children and before moving to a
small town filled with busybodies.

I realize that Sandra is probably an old friend to you, and perhaps because
of that friendship and history, I think perhaps you can't see that she's had
it in for Ned from the start, because she's met her match in him. She has
systematically attacked every thing he posts in order to get everyone up in
arms. Soon after her little war began, Joyce joined in (it seemed out of
loyalty to Sandra). If the two of them had just stopped their own tirades
about his so-called ranting, and had stopped bossing everyone around, the
whole flow of discussions would have soon returned to normal. Although I've
found her to be just as controlling and pushy in her opinions, I haven't
suggested banning her. I think banning should be reserved for the perverted
and violent. But if you are going to start banning those who flood the list
with posts of their own agendas and opinions, then perhaps she should be put
on a forced posting holiday as well. And after her, perhaps Joyce could have
a little leave of absence as moderator, and after her perhaps Tia or Mary?
Once you start banning people based on some vague sort of popularity contest,
you are heading down a very slippery slope.

Up until now I have been happy to think you were very cool headed and
objective about these stupid squabbles. When you named this list with a very
general name such as Unschooling, I would think you would have been inviting
all topics that even remotely relate to Unschooling.

If the list continues in the direction of banning dissenting views I suppose
I'll have to unsubscribe. I may be not much loss to the list, but it would
be sad for me to have to leave in order to live by my own ethical standards.

Thanks,
Sherry
