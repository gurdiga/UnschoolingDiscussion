Following this discussion is interesting. Just an intereting FYI, when i was taking a course for my Lactation Educator Certificate we learned that there is a scale from best to worse for baby...
1)mother´s milk from breast
2)mother´s milk from bottle or other feeding device
3)donated mother´s milk
4)goat´s milk with folic acid added
5) formula

Or, look at it this way: Goat milk is easier to digest and less
allergenic than cow's milk or soy milk --which is the base for
non-dairy formulas. So, if you cannot BF, might as well go with goat
milk.

Then again, I did neither. After I stopped nursing, I gave my kids
fortified rice milk because it is waaaaaaaaaaaaay more hypoallegenic
than goat milk. Goat yogurt has become "de rigueur" for most of our
cooking needs and I use Meyenberg Goat Milk for my coffee --became
sensitive to CM maybe after having Evan, certainly after Aidan was born.
