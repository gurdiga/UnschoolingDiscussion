I feel very sorry for the girl and her family but why was the alarm left on when
she was in the house anyway? Her mom had to know that this could be a
possibility. Didn't the girl hear the police car out front, then the knocking? I
understand that most kids are instructed not to answer the doors but if there
was a siren she probably could have looked out the window to verify that it was
the police or another emergency vehicle. I'm not defending the dog or the police
for this action. I also wonder why the police did not try to contact the mother
at work before entry. Maybe they believe it takes too much time, but I thought a
lot of security systems have a "checklist" of sorts to check prior to contacting
the police. The dog should also have been trained to "bark not bite" as long as
there is no weapon in the hands. The things that get me most about these kind of
articles are that they are only going to print enough to capture your attention
and get you upset about it so that the media can keep going with stories like
this.
