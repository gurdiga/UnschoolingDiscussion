this is precisely why, when asked what grade my 6 yr old is/would be in,
I hesitate. if i were forced to put him in an instituional educational
setting (IES--new acronym?), i would have kept him out of K last year (his
bday is in dec.) because his fine motor skills SUCK! His printing is
atrocious. Even I can't read it much of the time, no way a teacher could.

Of course, he comes by this "weakness" naturally.....*I* was kept off the
honor roll until penmanship was no longer a graded part of the curriculum
and eventually worked with an occupational therapist at jr. high age to
learn to write legibly. My dh, said child's father, writes like a 4th
grade boy, exactly as he has since he was in 4th grade and didn't ever
learn to form letters "correctly" (makes his circles backwards to this
day, and remembers getting his hand hit for it all those years ago).

However, outside of neatness, there is nothing in the K curriculum that
would challenge my son, nothing in the grade 1 curriculum, maybe some
reading in grade 2, and a little more in grade 3. But, if he were in most
typical IES's, his work would come back withthe words "REDO TOO SLOPPY)
written on it, regardless of the fact that he had just correctly
multiplied out 24x36 or diagrammed the circulatory system. I can only
tell his math problems (which btw, he chooses to do on his own
initiative--goddess only knows where *that* interest came from....not *my*
DNA that's for sure) are correct because I hear him say the answers or I
can ask him, "Is that a 2 or a 5?"
