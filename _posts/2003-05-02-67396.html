How about Bambi? His mother dies; there's a very artistically done
fire scene with crashing limbs and burning and injury.

But think about good ole fashioned fairy tales...there's plenty of
ugly, scary imagery in Grimm and Anderson, and even milder fairy
tales that don't have gore in them, have estrangement in families or
abuse from a parent to a child or from sibling to sibling or the
death of a beloved father...

When a Christian ministry came out a few years ago (after "Aladdin"
appeared on the screen) with a "Where are all the mothers? Disney
always leaves the mothers out of their movies." Hello? Cinderella
DIDN'T HAVE A MOTHER in the original fairy tale, you DOLTS! Neither
did Snow White or Pinnochio, and Briar Rose was raised by someone
other than her mother, and the list goes on and on and on and on.
Trying to make our world more...pure? safe? emotionally
stable?......As if this generation had a monopoly on wickedness and
scariness! urg.

That incident is what caused me to finally give up on trying to
monitor our movie viewing from ANYONE outside my own circle of
friends and family. I know what to ask friends, and they know what
our kids are able to handle.

peace, heidi
