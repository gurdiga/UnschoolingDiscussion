Btw, sometimes answers vary not based on mood but
expectations. People will answer according to what they think is
the "right" answer but it may not necessarily be the "true" answer
for them.

I've seen this happen a lot with the N/S and the P/J. And some
people are not as decidedly one characterisitic over the other
too.

To get a better reading, it's always more helpful to do the long
form Myers Briggs under the guidance of a therapist. The short
forms are usually enjoyable and offer some insight, but if you
read the description at the end of the test and it doesn't sound
like you, it's likely that you answered the questions from your
mask or "shoulds" rather than your truth. Read through the other
profiles until one clicks. That usually helps.
