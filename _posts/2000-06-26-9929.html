For everyone looking for "Z" recipies, I have one family one that I can
share (which is good if you have a lot of "maters" too) and one I found in
one of my cookbooks tonight.....

This is my grandfathers Tomato,Zucchini Casserole

2-3 Large Tomatoes Thinly Sliced
2-3 Z's Thinly sliced
1-2 Large white onions thinly sliced
1 stick of butter (ok so I didn't say it was low fat but its GOOD)
parmesan cheese (kraft grated is fine)
garlic salt

Preheat oven to 350 degrees.
In a medium casserole dish (with a lid) begin one layer of tomatoes, then
a layer of Z's, then a layer of Onions, top with a generous pat of butter,
then sprinkle (generous again) parmesan and garlic salt over top. Do as
many layers as dish will hold as ingredients will shrink while cooking. On
the top layer use a LOT of parmesan and double the butter you used on the
other layers. TRUST ME THIS IS WONDERFUL. Its hubbys favorite food. Bake
for 45 min at 350 degrees.


Ok...the next recipie is from one of my favorite cookbooks.....

Zucchini Sticks

475 degrees. Serves 6

1/2 c + 1 tbsp bread crumbs
1/4 c parmesan
1 1/2 tsp fresh oregano, finely minced
3 Med Zucchini
Olive Oil Spray
Freshly ground black pepper

Preheat oven to 475 degrees. Combine bread crumbs, Parmesan and oregano on
a plate. Cut squash into evenly sized spears. Put them on baking sheet in
one layer, spray them with olive oil. Turn them all over and spray again.
Roll each in crumb mixture and back to oiled baking sheet. Bake 8-10 min.
till brown. Serve Hot.

Hope someone can use these!
