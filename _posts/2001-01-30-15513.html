You have to be joking, right? If there was a pet over population problem
they would be used for human food.

Pet over population is not a myth but if it makes your live easier to live
in, well believe away to your heart's content.

And, btw, there are folks that believe in your method of pet population
control. The next time you are in the grocery store and pass by the pet
food section, check the ingredients listed on the labels of pet foods.
ANIMAL DIGEST is exactly what you say would be around if there were a
problem:

"Many pet foods use condemned parts, carcasses rejected for human
consumption (rancid, moldy), and the so-called "4D" animals- diseased,
dying, disabled or dead because they are obviously much cheaper. These meats
are "denatured", that is, sprayed with chemicals like kerosene to prevent
them from being sold as human grade. The pet foods are cooked at extremely
high temperatures to "sterilize" these meats. Dogs and cats, euthanized
using sodium pentobarbital (which survives the rendering process and will
still be present in the food), as well as roadkill turn up under the generic
names of "meat", "meat meal". What is skimmed off in the cooking becomes
"animal fat". Always be cautious if the manufacturer is unwilling to
specifically name the protein or fat source. Look for human-grade protein
sources to insure that your pet is not being fed cancerous tissue or meat
from a sick animal riddled with drugs."
http://www.greenworldpets.com/Evaluating%20Food.htm

Lynda
