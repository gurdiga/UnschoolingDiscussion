My first piece of advice for a new tat is: Get it somewhere *you* can see it
and enjoy it!!
I got my first tat (on my L ankle) at 25, I'd always known I'd get a moon
tattoo (diana=moon goddess) and I've never regretted it (of course, that tat
is how I got my favorite nickname: Nani Moon, from my best friend's little
guy - who is now 17 and not so little anymore!)
At DH's funeral #2, lots of *strangers* (they knew him, not me) handed me
cash saying, "In lieu of flowers" Now I don't know any Lou Flowers :::bg:::
but he sure did buy me a cool new tattoo :D Mitch and I had planned to get
matching Gemini/Monkey tats; an idea he quashed when he got sick, so I got
it on my back as a memorial for him. It's a beautiful tat, I only wish I
could SEE it!!
I got one for Hannah, over my heart, it's a symbol representing Bodhicitta
with her initials and dates... then I thought it wise to head off fate and
get one for Hayden - BEFORE he died :::vbg::: I got a calf tat of a tiger
(H) having his tail pulled by a monkey (me). The artist put some of Hannah's
ashes in the ink :D

Having said all that, implore her to be careful about what she chooses and
*who* she chooses to do the art!! My cousin got an angel tat on her back for
her gramma -- it is FUGLY (f'n ugly) and it's huge and there's no recourse
for a tat gone bad :'(

I don't buy the whole *addicting* spiel, what people witness (and then label
addiction) is the freedom from fear that is required to get the first ink,
then we're reminded of what it means to be fearless each time we get a new
one & when we don't care what people think of the ones we have :D Kinda like
an empowering spin on The Scarlet Letter :::vbg:::

Memorializing Sam this way will bring her comfort in ways unimaginable to
anyone else... your support here means the world to her <3
