Many years ago when I was a new unschooler I heard (something like)this
somewhere, and have quoted (something like) it a thousand times since:

"Suppose a successful college student graduates knowing one one-
billionth of all that there is to know. She probably knows less than
that, but let's just suppose that she graduated, knowing that much.

Who should decide which one-billionth she should learn? And does it
make any sense to push her really really really hard, so that she can
graduate knowing two one-billionths?"
