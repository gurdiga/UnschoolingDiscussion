My house is so quiet this morning. The nephew's are gone the nieces are
gone, the extra dog, the two extra cats.
After the fourth festivities we have no food left in the house and
there's a mountain of wet towels and I can't find the water sprinklers or
my good cake pans.

We went to a fireworks show in another town on the night of the third
because our little town doesn't do one. We spent the fourth eating and
playing and the kids put the trampoline right beside the pool and tried
to launch each other in. We had company and neighbors coming and going
until I couldn't keep track any more. We stayed up late on the fourth
and helped everyone else on our street set the neighborhood ablaze. One
of Kyle McIntyre's roman candles fell over and spun itself under our
truck for a wild fizzing display and the whole street cheered.

Since the tenth of June we went from a one kid household to a six kid
household and now back to one. Dylan had a wonderful time with all his
cousins and I don't know how I can make the rest of his summer stand up
to this last month, but we're planning a trip and some camping and hiking
and spelunking. I'm afraid nothing is going to seem as good as having
all those kids around.

Hope you all had a great Fourth. (and are recovering nicely)

Deb L
