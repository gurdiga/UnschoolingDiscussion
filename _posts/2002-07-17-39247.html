Hi, Mary!!

The other day we were cleaning the living room (going through those corners
in which things were neatly piled for future excavation), and I found a set
of cartridge pens with calligraphy nibs that someone gave me for Christmas.
Yes, yikes, been there since Christmas.

So I showed Holly how they worked, played with them a bit, and have wished
for several days that I had something to write! I keep thinking "I should
write a letter," but the people I actually need to write to have e-mail.

I could do the grocery list in my finest cursive, but my husband might rather
I'd just print it with arrows and notes about what size and all... (he does
the Costco shopping).

I could address envelopes, but we have nothing at the moment to send out.

I could write in a journal or diary, but that's all on the computer.

Post cards. I could send some post cards to my friends overseas, but every
one has e-mail, and I could sent attached photo-files.

So here I am, fully "schooled," no writing biases or fears, someone who
writes every single day, and I can't dig up a reason to use my handwriting
with my *new* pens.

Sandra
