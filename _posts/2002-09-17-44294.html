I could write something up, if it comes to that - but I think it's
important to *not* give the state any more than they're legally required
to get. I went and read some of your earlier posts, and looked up the
laws in Georgia, and they have no legal right to ask you for any of that.
You have to send a monthly attendance report, a yearly progress report
and a yearly declaration of homeschooling, and do standardized testing
every 3 years. You *don't* have to tell anyone whether you're
school-at-homing or unschooling, and you don't have to justify
homeschooling - and doing so just sets you up for more harassment, IMO,
plus it sets up other homeschoolers for harassment if they don't ive the
same kinds of information.. How about meeting them at the door with a
copy of Georgia ed regs
(http://www.home-ed-magazine.com/lawregs/georgia.html), and copies of #1,
#2, and #3 - and then telling her she doesn't have a right to any more
information?
