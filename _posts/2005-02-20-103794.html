With my 5 year old unschooling is fairly easy. She shows an intrest in something such as internal combustion engines ( our latest) and I help her explore and find answers for as long as that intrest holds.

My three year old son has autism and is nonverbal. He goes to preschool right now where he is directed through different preschool activities. This often requires teachers physicaly escorting him to his correct activity.
If he is left to himself he will spend the entire day chewing on his sock, possibly watching a video and running around the house aimlessly. He does not play with toys......a typical childs way of learning new things at this age.

I want him to reach his full potential and I really dont want to see him in public school past preschool but Im not sure how to go about unschooling him.

Nicole

Nicole,

Radically unschooling him might be the single most important thing you
could do for him. Seriously.

Unschooling doesn't mean leaving him to himself all day. Unschooling
has never meant that.

You should join Anne Ohman's list Shine With Unschooling. There are
lots of parents there with autistic children either who are radically
unschooling or who are asking the same questions you are.

http://groups.yahoo.com/group/shinewithunschooling/


~Susan McGlohn
