We're in Columbia, South Carolina.

It IS scary at first.

After you have deschooled yourSELF, *then* it's freeing. Until then, you're
still just bound up in that school/society brainwashing.

There's not a better way to GET to that point than to attend a conference.
There you can see unschoolers and their parents, hear inspring speakers, and ask
dozens of questions. You come away with a whole new outlook on learning and
children and unschooling. It's life changing!

Trust me!

Don't trust me---ask others! Or go to the website and read the testimonials!

Enjoy it? Almost guaranteed! It's a lot of fun--even if you refuse to learn
anything about unschooling! <g>

I hope to see you (and your friend) then.

I think just hearing about unschooling and what it is helps people to think
outside the box. Even if unschooling isn't what they are leaning towards it
lets people know that there is an alternative. There are so many people out
there that have no clue about unschooling. All they know is the "school at home"
model that seems to be everywhere and marketed so well.

I spoke with a group of new homeschoolers last night and, even though I
believe none will be unschooling, I think just hearing me speaking alongside
someone who is curriculum oriented made them think a little and there were some good
questions asked. I believe it just helped them to see someone who has a
different perspective. Maybe started them thinking.

Pam G.
