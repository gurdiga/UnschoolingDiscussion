The exhibition features hundreds of pieces of memorabilia from the epic film
trilogy including original costumes and jewelry, as well as the One Ring.

Not sure how young your kids are, but when I was in middle
school/high school my dad often took me down for musicals and we'd
spend the day. At that age what I loved most was that he'd let me
plan the agenda, so I looked at a good tourist's map of Boston and
picked the agenda, and we'd ride the T from place to place (I think
you can still get a day pass for like $5). My favorites were
browsing Faneuil (sp?) Hall, which is a big marketplace with
vendors, restaurants, etc. and lunch at Chinatown. The map also
shows "music district," etc., as far as shops. I'm not sure
what "inexpensive" is for you, as I consider the aquarium to be very
expensive for a family outing (it's our "big" summer outing we've
planned), but of course there's also the Boston Children's museum,
the Museum of Science (where Morgaine and I had a blast on our
special day out a few months ago -- another big expedition for us
since she'd been asking to see so many things from it), there's the
art museum, and lots of other things. The aquarium is easily a half-
day's trip at least for most kids taking their time. Really a good
tourist's map is your best bet though. Show it to the kids and see
what catches their eye!

Kristi

When thinking of taking in Boston sites, look to your local museum and zoo
memberships for ideas and reciprocity! In fact, check out the *connecting*
agency for a list of all the participating museums and zoos. The children's
museum here charges $70 for a family membership and the zoo is $60... on
line, I found a zoo in KS where the family membership is $40 and a
children's center in Pierre, SD with a $35 family membership; neither had
issue with taking money from someone who didn't live there. Both
memberships allow me to go locally AND nationally!!

One of our favorite things to do in Boston is the contemporary art
museum http://www.icaboston.org/Home (And it's free on Thursdays
after 5!).

The science museum is cool too. And remember, if you have a
membership to another science museum you can probably get into
Boston's science museum free. We have a membership at the Whitaker
center (Harrisburg) and got into Baltimore's and Boston's science
museums free. (I think you can get into the Boston children's museum
with the science museum membership too). For those with younger
children, the children's museum in Boston is AWESOME!

Boston's aquarium is okay. If you're used to going to Baltimore's
though, Baltimore's is better.

This summer (In July) I'd like to take my kids to Blue Man Group
show, which is supposed to be a fun show.

The Duck Tours are fun if you're new to Boston.

Sheila
