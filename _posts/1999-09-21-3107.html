The whole thread on late nights and spouses attitudes has definitely rung a
bell here. Part of my problem recently has been that I got depressed
(permanently tired, finding fault) since we moved house. I'm coming out of
it now, although there are other factors... But anyway, to cut a long story
short, I tend to 'hide' when I get that way, and the 'puter's a good way.
Now, I have offered to have the 'puter downstairs so I could
play/work/chat/whatever while dh watches tv, which is his 'thing' but no,
the typing would drive him crackers and it wouldn't look right (the
aesthetics thing's a whole other ball game, I won't go there just now!) But
we figured it out, and he knows that since we co-sleep, I don't get space at
all, so this is my time. If I want to go watch the late-night rerun of Star
Trek with him, I do. If I don't, I don't. We are both late night folks,
him more than me, but he works from home, so that's when he works. I'd get
up earlier for me-time if dd2 didn't wake up too, cos that's not me-time!

Tami, how wierd that he should suddenly change tune like this. Have you
read any of the Faber-Mazlish books? It's fairly basic psychology, but if
it works on kids, it ought to work on anyone, right? Can you find out
what's behind this using those kinds of tools? (particularly instead of
giving an argument, listening and affirming what he's telling you?) After
19 years, you perhaps think you ought to be able to read him like a book,
but we all have some hidden secrets - has something happened to dredge
something up from his own childhood? I don't know what else to say, except
that it does sound awfully like some medical problem, a food allergy
perhaps, I'm trying to steer clear of the obvious and scary brain tumour -
oops, I've gawn and done it now...

Anyhow, I'm not alone, you're not alone, we all seem to have similar
situations and we all solve them in our own ways, and in our own time, if
you'll excuse the pun!

I'm off to watch that Star Trek episode now!!
