I just found out that next year when I fill out a statement of
intention with the local public school for my now-6 year-old, I will
need to attach either an immunization schedule or a medical
exemption card or a religious exemption card. (no philisophical
exemptions in Iowa, yet that is what mine is) The next two children
have no immunizations and this seems to be a new law someone snuck
in as I haven't heard of this before. Does anyone know where I can
start looking into this as I am already worried and scared someone
is going to come and take my children away from me over this. (yes,
a fear I live with every day) My doctor has "allowed" me to skip the
vaccinations because after listening to me (and he did actually
listen) he said I was the only parent he met who actually knew WHY
she didn't want immunizations and he couldn't promise me I wasn't
right. However, a medical exemption would mean that he would have
to agree and sign a paper saying the immunizations
were "contraindicted" (please forgive spelling, I'm in a hurry,
don't tell me I spelled it wrong and make me feel bad, I've seen
that done on here at least once). Anyway, any websites that might
help me, any advice, and been-there-done-that stories? (and yes, I
will Google, too!) :o)
Mary in IA

Maybe one option to consider is to find a different doctor. Check at
your chiropractor's office to see if they know of a doctor who will
write you a medical exemption to vaccinations. A naturopath who is
an MD would do that, as would a MD who is a homeopath or anyone who
supports alternative medicine. I found two doctors who would be
willing to do that. One lives an hour and a half away, the other
lives 45 minutes away, but they're both worth the drive.
