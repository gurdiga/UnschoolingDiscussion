HENA 2008 Conference

Homeschooling = Infinite Possibilities!
Saturday & Sunday, March 1-2, 2008
LL Building, Arizona State University, Tempe, AZ
www.HENA.us <http://www.hena.us/> conference@...
<mailto:conference@...>





The Home Education Network of Arizona (HENA) is proud to announce our
2008 annual conference, again held on the campus of Arizona State
University in Tempe, Arizona in the LL Building.

This year, our Keynote Speaker is John Taylor Gatto, a former New York
Teacher of the Year, author and speaker. His talks will include:

*
Weapons of Mass Instruction (c)

*
War in the Shadows, the Silent Battle between Forced Attendance
Schooling and Open Source Learning (c)


Other speakers include Sandra Dodd, Unschooling Advocate, Pam
Sorooshian, Math Professor, Holly Craw, Covenant Home School Resource
Coordinator, Susanne Bain, local homeschooling author, Dale Barlett,
author of "Have Kids, Will Travel", David Niecikowski, Games Expert and
the list continues to grow. See the complete list at
http://hena.us/conferenceSpeaker.html
<http://hena.us/conferenceSpeaker.html>

Funshops include Fun Science Guys, Karate, Archery, Game Room and Video
Game Room. HENA Conference 2008 is a 100% family friendly conference
for all ages. With an excellent roster of speakers, panels and events
for all ages, you can meet, greet, learn and have fun with Arizona
Homeschoolers & Unschoolers!

Register Here! http://hena.us/conferenceRegistration.html
<http://hena.us/conferenceRegistration.html>

As some of you may know, the Memorial Union suffered a fire about a
month ago. Due to this fire, the conference had to be returned to our
original location from last year where everyone was happy to have good
parking and food options. We will have a mingling room as well this
year.

If you have any other suggestions or questions, please do pass them on
to conference@... <mailto:conference@...> at any time and this
will go to all the conference coordinators on the conference committee!
We're all volunteers, so feel free to volunteer as well! Visit
www.HENA.us <http://www.hena.us/> , email conference@...
<mailto:conference@...> or call 602-692-8923 for more information.

Please forward this email on to your friends, family, fellow
homeschoolers and post to relevant mail lists.
