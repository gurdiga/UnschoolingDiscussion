It's called pro-CHOICE, not pro-abortion. As in, I'm in favor of women
having the right to choose. I'm not in favor of telling everyone they
should have abortions. There's a world of difference between those two
statements.

I'm strongly pro-choice. I've also never had an abortion. Just because I
wouldn't choose to have one myself doesn't mean that I think it shouldn't be
available. I wouldn't have breast implants, either. But I'd rather it was
done under sterile medical circumstances than by anybody who thought they
could do it.

You're welcome to think of me as your enemy. But you should KNOW your
enemy. Not just the stuff that your friends provide you about them, but the
things they can tell you themselves. Listen to women here. We might have
different views, but we're real women who hold those views. Not the
stereotypes you might previously have held of us.
