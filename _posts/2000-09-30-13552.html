I think this is really unfortunate... my son is in Scouts, and he is
constantly pleading with his best friend to be in Scouts too. He was sooo
disappointed when Cody didn't show up at Scout Night. However, Cody has two
moms. They don't flaunt, but they don't make their arrangement a secret,
either. They are very involved in their kids' lives ... coaching soccer,
etc. I'm sure they simply don't want their son to be part of a group that
officially denounces his parents. I think that's understandable. The group
is missing out on a great family, though, and Cody is missing out on a great
experience that could really benefit him. For now I guess we'll just go
camping as two families, and try to figure out what to do morally in the
future, when Tony figures out why his friend feels excluded from this group
that is supposedly for "all boys."
