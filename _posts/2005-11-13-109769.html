I listened to an interview on the radio yesterday about artificial
intelligence and robots. Seems that there are two ways robots can
become "intelligent." One is a top-down method -- lines and lines of
computer programming giving the robot ALL the possible information it
could ever need to know. This is VERY limiting and nobody thinks this
is the direction that will really work, in the future, other than for
very limited purposes.
The other is building the ability to learn into the robot so that it
responds to "bumping into its environment" by creating its OWN
programming. This is the way real intelligence happens and the way
humans really learn, It is unpredictable and exciting.

UNSCHOOLED robots! YEAH!
