2007 Highlights: Sunday Cote' and Body Talk
Rethinking Education enthusiastically welcomes Sunday and her family for their second year ... and this most valuable technique for achieving wellness

Yes - that's her real name. No - she wasn't born on Sunday nor does she have a sibling named Monday. She does have a husband and two beautiful sons with completely normal names. They live together with their 3 cats, Henry, Monty and Lucy, (more normal names) in sunny Arizona. When not with her family or speaking and teaching metaphysics, Sunday used to spend her time just sitting and wondering how to fit it all in. Luckily since discovering BodyTalk she has found more time and energy to keep up with her exuberant and healthy boys! This is her second Rethinking Education Conference.
Sunday will host:
Tapping Into Your Family's Health
Need more energy to keep up with the kids? Want to help your family tap into their personal power to heal and stay healthy? Then join unschooling Mom and Certified BodyTalk Practitioner, Sunday Cote` as she shares the basics of the healing modality called BodyTalk. Sunday will offer demonstration sessions on participants as well as teach a technique that you can use with your family immediately to improve thinking ability, memory, concentration, stress reduction, and feelings of wellbeing.
As of 2006 BodyTalk has become the fastest growing health care approach in the world and is currently being taught in 27 different countries and in 8 different languages. BodyTalk incorporates the very best of energy medicine, science and medical knowledge, applied in a simple, safe and effective way to address the full spectrum of health. Its power is based on increasing the levels of internal communication within the body and stimulating the body's own ability to regain and maintain a healthy balance.
Sunday will also be available for private sessions during the conference at a special conference rate of $40 per session. You may make your appointment with her during the conference.

MORE 2007 Highlights: Sheila Murphy
RE welcomes Sheila with her great combination of unschooling and academic experience... and rebel approach to test taking

At the turn of the millennium, while living in Seoul, South Korea, our family - myself, my husband, Ray, our four kids -- fell into unschooling rather the same way that Alice fell into Wonderland. Seven years and a few countries later (Germany, England, and now, the USA), we're currently looking for the white rabbit in Lawrence, Kansas, which has turned out to be the biggest culture shock of all. In looking at the learning smorgasbord, the two older kids have done an eclectic mix of school classes, community classes, online classes, and didacticism. This led us to the college prep game, which our oldest child has decided to play. So, we've spent the last several months studying the rules of the game commonly referred to as college admissions, with special emphasis on "What Do You Really Need to Know to Take the ACT/SAT?" We found we had a bit of a strategic advantage, because prior to all of our overseas adventures, I had earned an M.A. and Ph.D. in rhetoric from the
University of South Carolina (where I also taught composition and literature). This has left me fairly fluent in academese.
Sheila will host:
Unschoolers and the ACT/SAT Writing Tests: No Problem!
Do "composition" and "compost" really have the same root? If so, just what is meant by "good writing"?
Explore this and other questions related to showing your stuff on the ACT or SAT writing section with minimal damage to self and sanity. This session is for students (and their parents) interested in taking the ACT/SAT as part of their pre-college preparation. They are, of course, not required, but they're also not as formidable as they've been made to appear.
