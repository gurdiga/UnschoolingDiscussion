Kandi,

Interesting that you mention this. When my son was in 6th grade they (the
teachers and school "counselor")ambushed us at a parent-teacher meeting and
after showing us some of Sean's artwork tried to tell us all these things
that were wrong with him... and how he was heading for trouble. He was
"aggressive" and had poor self-esteem, etc, etc.... well, what they were
getting at was that he would turn into one of those children we hear about
that hurts animals/pets or even, heaven forbid, a boy like the boys who did
the Columbine thing. No matter how much I told them that I, as his mother
and one who had done attachement parenting for his first 4 years, knew him a
whole lot better than that they wouldn't believe me. All that Psych stuff
and they thought they knew my son better.

Well, if they could only see him now... of course, he's afraid to draw
anything, or at least show us any of his work for fear of criticism, but
he's doing so much better self-esteem wise... and he cries, and always has,
if he sees an animal in pain or being hurt. It was the teachers and the
school that turned him the way they did. I'm now trying to take all that
out of him. It may take us a few years, but I'm willing to give it that
time... now if only others would stop asking him when he's going back to
"school" we might have more success here.

I'm sorry about the long post. Sometimes I just tend to get carried away...
especially when I start thinking about the way my "baby" was treated.

Jeanmarie

Hi! I am new to unschooling groups. I can relate to JeanMarie's story of
the unnecessary low self-esteem they(public school) place on children. I
had a son that we had homeschooled and decided to place him in public school
for his 2nd grade - BIG MISTAKE! He was in ps for 6 weeks and the school
decided he was doing well in all subjects except reading . They were
unwilling to provide him with the necessary help and were wanting to place
him in first grade. Needless to say- we yanked him. My now 3rd grader, who
probably reads at a late3rd, early 4th grade level, feels that he doesn't do
well at ps.
The reason I joined unschooling was to see if there are some Minnesota
members that might be able to give me some insight and instruction about
legalities and records and such. We are currently residing in Missouri but
will be moving in approximately 2 months to the Minneapolis area. If there
is someone out there with advice - please send it my way. Thanks. Kate
