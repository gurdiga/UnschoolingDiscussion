Finish this sentence - To me, unschooling is...


I know I could get so long winded on this one and when I read everyone
else's, I'll be going "Oh yeah, that too!!!" I'll try to be plain and
simple, which will be a first.

Unschooling to me is the freedom to go at one's own pace, no matter how slow
or fast that may be according to someone else. No matter what one choses to
busy themselves with if anything at all. To find one's own way, no matter
where, when, how and why, as long as one is happy and respectful that others
have that same freedom as well.

Mary B

Unschooling is... Riding the wind, eating cake for dinner, digging the yard
up in search of dinosaur bones cause someone in a town close by found some in
their back alley, listening to the music in the rain, spending way too much
time on the computer with you guys, looking for fairies at dusk, spending the
day cutting and gluing and listening to Mom's old albums (Mom explaining
about 45's to disbelieving kids), going to the store and sitting down in the
produce area to look at ugli fruit, explaining things to friends and
neighbors, running nekkid through the sprinkler, in the middle of the night,
when it is too hot to sleep, playing with magnets, planting a garden,
dressing in a Spiderman costume every day for a week eight months after
Halloween, reading a book late into the night cause you can't put it down,
sleeping till noon cause you don't have to be anywhere, picking that book
back up again two years or two hours later and reading it again, explaining
things to friends and neighbors again, eating cake for breakfast, popping a
bowl of popcorn for the ducks, walking by the river at dawn to see the
cranes, spending a day playing playstation, painting rocks, having the skate
park all to ourselves, explaining to the truant officer who patrols the mall
every day that the kids really truly don't have to be in school right now,
dancing to the music in our heads, the distinction of being the weird family
on the block, the distinction of being the wierd family in the family,
chalking up the street to make our own game of human tic-tac-toe cause there
isn't enough people for checkers or chess and too many cars go by to play
Monopoly, living life.

~Nancy
