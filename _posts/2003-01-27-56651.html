Hi all,
I too was drawn to the 'learn more World History from stories'
especially in the timeline format.

I too cringed at the structure, intensity and knew that I would never
do all of it.(I love to read,hate to write, so how can I make my kids
do it ). Maybe a more Charlotte Mason style wpuld fit but...
That "teach kids how to think" never made sense to me either.
So, because I never had any History except Columbus to The Civil War
(over and over and over), I will learn about World History. Maybe I
will train or untrain my mind but it is mine...theirs is theirs. My
kids do not need to suffer for what I think I missed.

I went to the NC conference. Jessie scared me.
I am not sure that I want to go to the MD one unless there will be a
big comfy hangout area where unschoolers and want to bees can joyfully
NOT attend the lectures!!

Beth in Md
Who finally went off what little was left of "school at home" and
never went "back" after Christmas vacation...Yahoo!!!
