>Feb 26-28: National Public Radio will be turning the ears of the
>nation toward homeschooling!
>
>Beginning Monday, February 26, and continuing through Wednesday,
>February 28, National Public Radio's program "All Things Considered"
>will look at homeschooling through the lives of three families in
>three different American cities. This story is part of "The Changing
>Face of America" series and will be told through the actual words of
>the family members.
>
>Sean Collins, a senior producer for NPR News, followed three
>homeschooling families for several days, recording their activities
>and conversations. He chose families with diverse approaches to
>homeschooling to give listeners a sense of the breadth of the
>homeschooling community, including:
>-- An Indiana family that homeschools primarily for religious reasons
>using a structured approach,
>-- A Texas family that homeschools in order to meet the special
>educational needs of their child, and
>Â–- An Oregon family that homeschools because it allows them to use
>the whole community as their classroom.
>
>Check with your local NPR affiliate for air times.
>
>NHEN volunteers were pleased to be instrumental in providing
>homeschooling contacts and background information to NPR as it
>planned this program. For more information check out NHEN's web site
>at http://www.nhen.org/ or NPR's web site at http://www.npr.org/
