Just a little further south (less than 30 minutes) is Venice Beach - famous
for shark's teeth! You can just walk along the beach and practically scoop
them up. We haven't been in a few years (We live in Cape Coral, a little
further south yet), but I hear that it's still like that.

it's been years since we've been, but the public school kids do field trips
there, and I'm sure it would be possible to set something up - a tour, or
maybe they're open to the public. If you can't find anything about it, let

Sam,

The one in Sarasota is the first weekend of March (Thursday - Sunday), on the
grounds of the Ringling Museum, right on Hwy. 41. There's another one in
Largo (near St. Pete) in April, and it runs for like 5 or 6 weekends. We've
never been to that one, but have heard it's really good. The Sarasota one is
our absolute favorite thing to do - we love it!!!
