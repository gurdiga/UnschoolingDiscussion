I see the same things in the kids around us, but not in Chamille.
She doesn't feel stupid. Sometimes things get her down, but mostly
it's circumstantial, and external, not internal, so she finds ways
to "snap" out of it.

I've found that when you have that internal strength of character
from having a healthy self respect, you can ward off those icky
things easier and bounce up and be your happy self quicker. On the
occasions that I've seen one of my kids get "down" about something,
when I talk about it with them, it is never internal, always
fleeting, they just know how to resist these negative things that we
encounter throughout life. I owe that to the way they know
themselves deeply and are respected and encouraged in their lives.
