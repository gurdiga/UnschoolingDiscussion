One way to Make a Time Line:

Materials
8 pieces of poster board
10 pieces of two different colors of construction paper
( I used yellow for BC and blue for AD )
3 different color markers
( I used black and orange for BC and black and green
for A.D. )
Glue

Directions:
1. Draw six lines three inches apart down each piece of poster board.
2. Draw eight lines three inches apart across each piece of
poster board.
3. Draw 8 -1 inch lines across construction paper.
4. Cut out 1 inch construction paper strips. Fold each strip in half and cut
again. 5. Glue the construction paper strips on the 6 lines you made on the
poster board. 6. Glue one more line of construction paper strips on the
bottom of each piece of poster board.
7. Now you have 6 construction paper lines on a piece of poster board. Do
this for each piece of poster board.
8.Use the black marker to write the dates at the top of each square on your
poster board to read up and down.
9.At the top of the first piece of poster board for each
set of four boards I labled one B.C. and one A.D.

Here is an example:

B.C. ( Before Christ )
0 |0 |0 |0
0 |0 |0 |0
0 |9 |8 |7
3 |2 |2 |2
______________________________________________

10. Use the color other than black to mark the straight up and down lines.
On the example above I used orange
marker for the lines infront of the zero. I didn¹t make the lines dashed I
drew them straight. I just can¹t make
them straight on the computer. I also wrote the years so you can
read them only on their side, which I couldn¹t do on the computer.
