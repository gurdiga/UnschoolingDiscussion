Myranda,
I hope that I am not throwing cold water on your idea of the
Mennonites but we lived around 4 groups of them and I never heard any of
them say they did not believe in spanking. In fact Train Up A Child was
making the rounds there. I know that children were spanked as we had
discussions on it...albeit rarely as they are very private people.
About their children never crying...I did not see that either. They are
"taught" very young not to cry loudly.
They do cuddle and take time for their children probably more than the
norm and are for the most part gentle and loving but there is still "do
as you are told>" The feeling of community is very strong and if you do
not abide by the community rules the alternative can be more than most
can handle.
As for them being the truest christians around (my wording not yours)
may come from our looking in at a group of people that we rarely get to
see behind closed doors.
They have there fair share of in fighting and petty problems...they are
just as human as you and I.
One of the things I always thought interesting is they do not tell their
children when a new baby is expected. They still keep it between the
adults. Where as "english" (as we are called) are so excited we tell the
postman, librarian and the store clerk.....we do live in different
worlds.One not always being better than another , just different.


This is from my neck of the woods though in Tn. and Ky.
sharon

Hi Sharon,
Where 'bouts in TN? The Mennonite group I personally know and spent a good bit of time with is in Lobelville (sp?). Over a 3 yr time period, I occasionally worked with them, ate with them, ran errands for them, took care of animals with them, and once helped with a barn-raising. I was fortunate to have had friends who were well-known and trusted in the community, and they accepted me quickly. I made quite a few good friends while I was there. Their way of teaching the children to behave was that when the child did something "unacceptable" everyone (including other children) would turn their backs or otherwise ignore the child until he/she quit whatever it was. The mothers carried their babies in slings everywhere until the child was old enough (usually around the age of 2 or 3) to follow and keep up, and then the boys usually went with their papa, while girls stayed with mama.

Yes, I picked up on that not telling about new babies too, and actually had a very hard time telling others about my first two pregnancies!
Myranda
