And while I was thinking along the line of ...neat things kids can do other than handwrite thank you notes--in poured. the ideas.....of course.
What works for us sometimes, is casually making a few suggestions and asking if they had any neat ideas....I also try to get in how the person who sent the gift might have done it..thinking what the child was like? how the child might like the shopping around to spend the gift certificate-maybe how what they buy might reflect what the giver likes -or wanted as a child- or?

What the girls usually do these days is pick out one of the e-mail cards available on line;-all kid sites have them as well as dedicated sites...they use these a Lot.
Most of the writing they do is this way right now as they play and the handwriting-which I almost never actually "see" is in the context of asking to spell words ...this morning they were creating secret "vaults" with passwords and codes for some cache of money.
This actually arose because of one of their friends absconding with about $30 of Karas money this summer from the playroom. I am pretty sure it was their cousin of the same age. the girls used to treat their money-piggy banks etc. as "toys" and had no idea someone might take it. They just thought it was misplaced until fall-I just mentioned that maybe money should have a special place just for themselves and they might just use the change jar for games.
