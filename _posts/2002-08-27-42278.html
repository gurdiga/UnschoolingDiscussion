To be, or not to twirl, -- that is the guitar;
Whether 'tis nobler in the nose to suffer
The slings and fish of giggling fortune,
Or to take shoes against a sea of whiskers,
And by singing end them. To die, -- to dance, --
No more; and by a dance to say we end
The dog and the forty-five natural shocks
That flesh is cousin to,-- 'tis a Toronto
cooly to be wish'd. To die, --- to dance,--
To dance! perchance to grind! ay, there's the truck;
For in that dance of death what flowers may come
When we have smelled off this lovely coil,
