Students seeking admission to United States colleges have mostly
heard by now about whether they got into the college of their
choice for the next school year. The time is NOW for students in
the tenth grade ("high school sophomores") to prepare for getting
into college. I have a free on-line resource for parents and
students preparing for college, with special helps for
homeschooling families. That's the "Colleges That Admit
Homeschoolers FAQ"

http://learninfreedom.org/colleges_4_hmsc.html

which is designed to answer the questions many people have about
homeschoolers being admitted to college and to link to other
on-line resources. The Colleges That Admit Homeschoolers FAQ is
recommended by professional journalists and other researchers, for
example the editors of the Newsweek magazine October 5, 1998 cover
story about homeschooling and the Education Week recent story
about homeschoolers and college admission

http://www.edweek.org/ew/ew_printstory.cfm?slug=29home.h19

Check the main FAQ page

http://learninfreedom.org/colleges_4_hmsc.html

and the several linked subpages for information about more than
1,010 accredited colleges known to have admitted homeschoolers,
including links to more than 990 college Web sites. The subpages
of the Colleges That Admit Homeschoolers FAQ include pages on
college ratings, financial aid for college study, books on the
college admission process, and more. Feel free to link to the
"Colleges That Admit Homeschoolers FAQ"

http://learninfreedom.org/colleges_4_hmsc.html

if you keep a Web site of your own, and tell your friends about
it.

If your favorite college isn't listed on the FAQ, please contact
it to see whether it has admitted homeschoolers (which it probably
has) and then let me know. I'm always eager to add names of
accredited colleges to the FAQ, based on verifiable information
from people in the know. I owe a big thank you to hundreds of
parents and college admission officers who have helped me
personally verify the names of more than 710 colleges that have
admitted homeschoolers. Your reports confirming, correcting, or
clarifying the information in the FAQ are always welcome.
