Here's a list of some of our favorites, in no particular order. Of course,
you'll have to decide for yourself if any of these are sexist, religious or
violent.

Charlotte's Web by E.B, White
Stuart Little by E.B. White
Mr. Popper's Penguins by Richard Atwater
The Henry Huggins books by Beverly Cleary
The Ramona books by Beverly Cleary
The Magic Treehouse series by Mary Pope Osborne
Flat Stanley by Jeff Brown
Invisible Stanley by Jeff Brown
Bunnicula by ? Howe
Pippi Longstocking by Astrid Lindgren

Happy reading.

My kids liked a book called Backyard Dragon.

It wasn't the highest level of eternal literature, but it was fun.

Harry Potter, if you haven't done that one.

They enjoyed Baby Island. Perhaps it's sexist in that it's a fantasy about
taking care of babies after a shipwreck (and girls save the day). But it's
fun. If he's not interested, it doesn't mean he's sexist, just that that's

lmkenney888 at 2001-12-19 05:49:37 +0200 said:

You could try Peter Pan and The Water Babies by J.M. Barrie; The
Cricket in Times Square by George Selden; Dr. Doolittle; Charlotte's
Web and Stuart Little by E.B. White; James and the Giant Peach and
Charlie and the Chocolate Factory by Roald Dahl; the Little House on
the Prairie series by Laura Ingalls Wilder; The Moffats by ?Estelle
Estes. We (my 5.5yodd and I) also like books by Marcia Williams -
The Adventures of Robin Hood, The Iliad and the Odyssey, Tales from
Shakespeare, and the Asterix and Obelix series by Goscinny and
Uderzo, all in comic book format and some more historical than
others, although not always gentle.
