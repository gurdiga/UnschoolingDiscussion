when I first started with the tea ds wasn't into it,
either, but I told him it was like medicine. It was
"feel better" tea. Like vitamins. He didn't really
like it, I don't think, or maybe he was just being
contrary, who knows, but I just told him it wasn't an
option. It was like taking a spoonful of yucky
medicine, only it was taking 6 sips of tea.... then a
little bit later 6 more sips...then I really got into
this honey kick (honey garlic and onions...I'm very
into them)...and started telling him there was alot of
honey in the bottom...so he would take bigger "sips"
to get there. Sometimes, he would take 6 and then I
woudl take 6 (very little ones for me...leaves more
for him) and now he just drinks it. Loves it. Dd (3)
doesn't like anything near her mouth that is warm (a
bit of a burn-fear freak that way) and so I started
her with making up double strength hot tea and putting
some honey and ice in her sippy cup thing and then
pouring the tea over them and shaking it up til the
ice melted and it was a nice room temperature drink
that was sweet and she gobbled it down like juice.
Also you could make iced tea or sun tea (some non
caffenated one) and just add a couple sleepytime bags
in with the mix, serve it with dinner. Just a few
suggestions.
-Bobbie
