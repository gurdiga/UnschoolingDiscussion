<<or tell them to go elsewhere because they aren't "real" unschoolers>>

I didn't suggest the poster go elsewhere. Just asked if she was sure this
was the list she was looking for. Lots of people confuse relaxed
homeschooling with unschooling. How will they know if someone doesn't point
it out?

Some people went to a diner and asked the waitress for a chocolate shake.
The waitress comes back with vanilla shakes all around and announces "Here
is your chocolate shake." All the calling it chocolate in the world isn't
going to make it change from vanilla, it is missing a vital ingredient. The
two have lots of similar ingredients but a chocolate shake and a vanilla
shake are definitely different. Not saying one is better, not saying one is
worse, but they are different.

When people come to a place to discuss chocolate shakes, I don't think it is
inappropriate to point out when someone is actually talking about a vanilla
shake and ask them were they aware this place is about discussing chocolate,
particularly when there are a gazillion other places to discuss vanilla and
a very limited number to discuss chocolate.
