Hello Pam,

I was labeled math excelled in K and left my happy teacher and room with 1
other smart guy to be drilled with 2-3 grade math. I missed story time and
free play. Got back to the room for recess and nap. For the whole year. Sad.
I knew my math facts because I snuck into my big brother and sister's books
and played math and school when I was 3-4 years old. I have a photographic
memory, so I looked smart to the Kindergarden teacher and voila, my path was
set for me. I have since always fely uncomfortable with language arts. I was
math smart, right? They even thought I was reading delayed in 1st grade and
tested me. Hello, I missed reading in K, make any sense. So, for the rest of
my ps career, I felt dumb and brilliant. Weird for self-esteem. I got by
doing minimum always. I was AP math and finished community college calculus
my senior year of HS. I had to redo english again and again. I'm still
affected I think, from this early label of math smart.

In college I found plants and my passion. I picked a career in horticulture.
I'm finally comfortable because I know my strengths. I'm great at what I do
and I love it too, though since kids I really do not work for money. I
usually just get asked all kinds of plant/gardening questions from my
friends and their spouses and anyone else, because I love to talk plants and
anyone who asks is going to get me going.

Mary
