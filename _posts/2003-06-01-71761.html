>I don't feel as if anything these people do in their personal lives is any of
>my business.

I don't think anyone really thinks it's anyone's business but the people
involved, as in, something we have or could have influence over. What I do
think is that we, as human beings, react negatively to things happening to
other folks that we would not want to happen to ourselves (ie, be abandoned
in a most-public manner by our spouses, have a daughter kidnapped, etc) so
in a feeling of solidarity with the injured party, we admonish the doer of
the negative actions and the negative action itself in story-form.

I think part of us yearns somewhat for the story-teller mode of information
in transmitting social mores, responsibilities and 'warnings' to each
other. Actors and other media darlings are well-known, and so the stories
that surround them are more easily accessible (and retained?) than would be
a story about my cousin Steve, who nobody's met or is interested in.

In another way, what some media darlings do in their personal lives where
it transects professional lives, *does* affect 'my business'. (ie, putting
personal 'buddies' into positions of authority where they can do great
harm.) When that happens, you can be sure that I make it my business and
actually try to have influence over what is going on (or make some noise
about it at least!)

>They are harassed daily by the media and the things done to them
>would be ILLEGAL if they were done to us.

Like what?
I can't think of any legal harassment that could happen to them that would
not also be legal if it happened to little-old-me.
