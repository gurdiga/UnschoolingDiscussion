As far as other unschooling books:

**I just thought of another one that really grabbed me when I read it about 7 years ago. It's called Real Lives- eleven teenagers who don't go to school by Grace Llewellyn. I read it when my daughter was 3 and was just amazed at all the possibilities for older kids.**

Yes! I just remembered the same thing with Teenage Liberation Handbook. Although my kids are only 10, 8 and 6, I read Teenage Liberation Handbook a couple of years ago. It had so many ideas that would helpful for anyone who is unschooling. It helped me in deschooling my thinking.

It also really gave me alot of information on how to answer questions from others who don't get how unschooling can move right into adult life.

I learned alot from that book. More than I even realized.
