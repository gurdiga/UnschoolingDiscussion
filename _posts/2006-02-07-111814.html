Hummers & Howlers with Cynthia Reynolds &
The Organization for Bat Conservation

Friday, March 3, 2006
9:00 am to 12:00 pm
Kellogg Hotel & Conference Center- East Lansing, MI

Featuring -

Nature Writing with Cynthia Reynolds-

This year's program features Cynthia Reynolds, award-winning
journalist and author of the new children's book The Far- Flung
Adventures of Homer the Hummer. The students will explore the world
of nature writing as Ms. Reynolds shares with them the process of
writing her children's story book. MAS will be selling her book
and Ms. Reynolds has graciously offered to provide each family with
a free educational teacher's guide. For more information about the
book and author please visit www.mittenpress.com/.

&

Creatures of the Night with the Organization for Bat Conservation-
Students will learn about creatures of the night from the
Organization for Bat Conservation as they discover the most
fascinating features and adaptations of many nocturnal animals
including bats, flying squirrels, owls, and more. Live animals will
be shown including bats, owls, and a few surprises. To find out
more about the work of OBC please visit www.batconservation.org.
