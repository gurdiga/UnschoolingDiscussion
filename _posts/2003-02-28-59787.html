Here's the list in published order. The first ones are about school reform
and the later ones show his realization that it wouldn't work because
children didn't have the free choice to be there.

1964 - How Children Fail, by John Holt (Pitman Publishing)

1967 - How Children Learn, by John Holt (Dell)

1969 - The Underachieving School, by John Holt (Dell)

1970 - What Do I Do Monday?, by John Holt (Dell)

1974 - Escape from Childhood, by John Holt (Dell)

1976 - Instead of Education, by John Holt (E.P. Dutton)

1981 - Teach Your Own, by John Holt (Delacorte Press)

1989 - Learning All the Time, by John Holt (Addison-Wesley)

1990 - A Life Worth Living: The Selected Letters of John Holt, edited
by Susannah Sheffer (Ohio State Univ. Press)

1997 - Growing Without Schooling: A Record of a Grassroots Movement
(Holt/GWS)

28 Feb 2003, at 11:07am
My personal preference is How Children Fail. It has so many real life examples demonstrating WHY certain methods seldom work.
It is also from a viewpoint of JH changing his own assumptions because of his experiences. Provides good reasons to examine one's own ideas and move toward thinking in a different manner.

In my opinion, How Children Learn, was a little more "theoretical".

Escape from Childhood and Instead of Education are a little more "out there" for a "beginner", although when I re-read them years later they made MUCH more sense. I always compared the change in my reactions to be similar to the change from my early ideas that I "might" breastfeed for as "long" as six months to deciding that weaning at six months was very, very, early.
