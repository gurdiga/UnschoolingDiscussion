anyone ever hear of this book? It is featured in Reader's Digest this
month (June issue) about a child who has been labelled as Learning
Disabled, who found herself in ice skating, when she was (???) five I
think. Quite young, at any rate.

I found it an intriguing story, how the poor mother saw the
difficulty the little girl had sitting still and listening in her
(get this) second year in NURSERY SCHOOL????? gag By the time this
poor kid was five, she had been rejected by every kindergarten,
special ed school, and some of them because she wasn't disabled
ENOUGH (i.e. no physical probs, no obvious mental/intelligence probs.
Just inability to learn what was appropriate from what wasn't.)

And then a friend of her mom's says "Take her ice skating" and the
kid blossoms. the website shows a confident and beautiful teenaged
girl wearing jeans and a jacket, holding skates in her hand.


anyway. I'd love to know how the story ends. Guess I better hie me
off to the library.
