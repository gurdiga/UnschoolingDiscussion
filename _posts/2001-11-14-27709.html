So in families in which stress from school or other factors has kids keyed
up, adding stress about the symptoms won't help. I was blamed for my excema,
and was limited in foods (it never helped once, but my mom followed doctor's
orders anyway without any evidence of success), in what I could wear (which
is fine--cotton instead of the then-popular nylon), in what I could take a
bath in (bath oil instead of soap, which is comfortable but not all that
helpful with skin problems in the desert!!). Being told if I wouldn't
scratch it it would go away never helped. It was as if I was allergic to my
own sweat when I was stressed, and the stresses in my life caused sweatiness,
and I'd break out.

Dietary considerations are great as concerns general health and digestion,
but just to identify a culprit and eliminate it completely and dramatically
as though it's the one magic switch is not the same as general health, and I
know this from experience.

When I moved away from my parents, I rarely had excema again (finals time at
college would trigger it). So either I grew out of it or I moved away from
it.

None of my kids have had any.
