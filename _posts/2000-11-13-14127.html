> We are a homeschooling family in Sweden with three children 6, 10, and
> 12 who are having big problems with the local school
> authorities. We are now facing a fine of 40 000 skr ($4500/£ 2700) if we
>
> don't send our children to school right away. We are not
> asking for your money, but rather for your help in emailing the
> authorities in Sweden and asking them to withdraw the fines and let
> people homeschool.
>
> In the meantime we have been forced to flee our home to another school
> district within Sweden where they hopefully are more
> tolerant of homeschooling. But we miss our home and our life, and we
> want to move back to our farm. And we are not alone.
>
> Friends of ours have already had to move within Sweden. Homeschooling is
>
> legal in Sweden and we have the same laws for the
> whole country but they are just not interpreted in the same way in every
>
> legal district. So people have to move to different parts of
> Sweden because of their beliefs. Other homeschooling friends of ours
> have just fled to the UK since they didn't feel safe in their own
> home. We miss them a lot already.
>
> The Swedish Minister of Education has just, in a TV program, expressed
> that she wants to make it even more difficult to
> homeschool in Sweden in the future by changing the laws. This will mean
> that we will see more refugees from Sweden because of
> their beliefs as the interest for homeschooling increases.
>
> So what we are asking you about is your support in helping us to protect
>
> our right to homeschool in Sweden, and to guarantee every
> Swedish child the right to homeschool if they desire, now and forever.
> Please help the Sweden authorities understand that
> homeschooling is an excellent way of educating children and that you are
>
> aware of the persecution of homeschoolers in Sweden.
>
> Below is a list of people or places you could email on our behalf with
> some suggestions what to write about. A short but personal
> note to one or all of them below is perhaps best. Please express your
> feelings in a friendly and non-aggressive fashion. In all
> messages please include something like:
>
