Sharon,

We got the plans off the 'net. Try googling homemade rat cage maybe? There
are many sites. Look around 'til you find the one you like.

We used the shelving you can find at Lowe's or Home Depot. It's not "wire"
exactly, but it's often used in closets, mostly white---I don't think I saw
any colors. You must cut ut with a handsaw, or maybe a dremel would work. The
shelves are 6' long. We cut them in half. Do all your measuring in the store!


I got one of those under-the-bed storage boxes for the base---throw the top
away. Make sure the shelving fits side-by-side and in the corners of the
base.

Four 6' shelves will make two double sides (Length), two single sides
(Width), and a top with a lip that measures 32" X 16" X 35" (they overlap in
the corners) with one extra piece that can be cut up and used for ramps and
perches if you're clever and handy (we didn't!<G>). We attached everything
with some neat fasteners from the hardware department. The lid is attached
with those plastic things men love to connect stuff with (I don't know what
they're called: you thread one end into the other which has a hole, and pull
tight?)). You can add "stories" by attaching more shelving. We also have a
rope and a hammock. Some families cut doors into the sides for easy access;
we just lift the top and they scurry up.

Kelly
