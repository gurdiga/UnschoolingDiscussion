This point, that Jews who have converted to Christianity are
not "Jews", is well taken. Their religious beliefs now come from
Christianity, not Judaism.

I don't think anyone was intending to state that a person can embrace
Judaism and Christianity all at the same time. I believe the
misunderstanding comes from the possible definitions of Jew:
religious and ethnic. An ethnic Jew (genetically speaking, born of a
ethnically Jewish mother) who has converted to Christianity is still
an ethnic Jew, right?

So my understanding of the phrases "converted Jew" or "completed Jew"
is that they refer to ethnic Jews who are born-again Christians (from
a religious standpoint).

I don't know what Jews believe about being chosen, but Christians
believe that ethnic Jews are a race that was/is chosen by God
(Deuteronomy 7:6). We also believe that when some Jews decided not
to follow him or to believe that Jesus is the messiah, he added
Christian believers to his chosen people (I Peter 2:9-10 and
others). This is why Christians have (or should have) high respect
for the traditions of Judaism.
