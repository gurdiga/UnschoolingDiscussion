Do you consider yourself an unschooler? Are you willing to let a reporter
visit you to find out what a typical day is like? If so, your 15 minutes of
fame may be at hand.

I have been talking with Peter Kilvorn of the New York Times. He is
intrigued by the idea of unschooling and would like to find out more for an
article he is writing. What he really wants to do is to spend some time with
a family to see what kind of things go on during a "typical" day. I've
explained to him that there is no such thing as a typical unschooler or a
typical unschooling day, but he is hoping to catch one of those "aha!"
moments when a kid gets excited by something they are doing or finally
catches on to something they have been trying to understand.

He assures me that he is not out to blast unschoolers but just wants to
present unschooling as an interesting alternative to other learning
approaches.

Peter Kilvorn is in the Washington D.C. Bureau of the New York Times, but is
willing to travel outside the area if necessary. If you would like to
contact him for more information or to offer your family for an interview,
you can call him at (202) 862-0361, or e-mail him at PeKilv@...

He is interested in doing something soon, so please do not respond to this
after May 27, 2000. Also, he is only interested in unschoolers, NOT
homeshoolers in general, so don't respond unless you consider yourself an
unschooler.
