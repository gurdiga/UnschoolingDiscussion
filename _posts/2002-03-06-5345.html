There are three conferences coming up at which I'm speaking, and others here
are speaking at one or another of them too. So I thought maybe it would be
good to ask this thoughtful group for suggestions on what is, would be, has
been helpful to you at an in-person presentation.

Different people like different things too. Some like a workshop at which
they're directly addressed, and given something to hold or do or say. Others
like anonymity where they listen in quiet stillness (on the outside, anyway).

Is there anyone on this list who was in Long Beach last spring when I did the
thing with the kitchen timer and the six topics which were ended at ten
minutes by the timer? I'm thinking of doing that one again, maybe.

I'm thinking that in South Carolina I should do the "That's Not Educational"
game that I've done sometimes before, where people name things and I tell
them what learning opportunities could be made of them. If I can't, they get
a prize.

Are there other conferences than these three that some of you are attending
or participating in?

Saturday, April 6, 2002, Bay Shore, Long Beach, California

August 17-19, 2002, HSC Home=Education Conference Sacramento, California

Oct 11, 12, 13, 2002, Live & Learn Conference in Columbia, South Carolina.


Sandra

P.S.
For those who might be here who are or were involved in the cooperative
MindFull conference in September in Fort Worth, I've withdrawn from
participation. I'm sorry it didn't work out differently. Before I signed on
I was told it would be open to others up to the last minute, for locals who
found out about it late. Then it was closed entirely. There were a few
other problems, which perhaps could just be written off as having to do with
my own pushy personality, but I'm not going to subject five kids to a drive
into the heat of Texas for that (not to mention hotel meals for six people
for three days, gas, risking lives, etc.) And the teenaged boys were SO
realistic about the "opportunity" to meet lots of other homeschooled teens.
They said if they liked them a lot it would be frustrating that they wouldn't
get to see them more, and if they didn't like them much it would be
frustrating that they'd gone so far and were stuck there three days. Pretty
smart kids.
