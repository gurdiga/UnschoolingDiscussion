Basically it is the same....
We hatched out two the first time.   The dog killed one by accident (I forgot about those long necks sticking out)   and we took the other one back to it's mom who had hatched the others so it wouldn't be alone.  Then we hatched out 3 more.   One was killed when it was several months old...I think it was sick and it wandered off, and we couldn't get it in at night, something got it.  Then one died..compacted crop..I think it was poke berries.  So we have one left.  He is our pet.   His name is Peaky.  He thinks he lives at the house even though we try to convince him he is supposed to be at the barn.

27 Mar 2000, at 9:52am

Sonia,
It has been a great experience for my kids, and for us too.
We bought baby chicks the first few times, but we had full grown chickens we had bought.  So, we have a coop and a pasture for them when they grow up.
For the eggs you need an incubator until they hatch, and then a brooder (the box with shavings) for the chicks until they get a bit bigger, then you should have a place to move them outside.
We have been very lucky to have to experience lots of animals because we have lots of space to have them.
Yep, we do have a pet peacock.  He lives in the barn.  But, he thinks he should live up at our house.
Ann
