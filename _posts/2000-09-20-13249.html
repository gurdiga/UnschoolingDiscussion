Hello unschoolers,

I am Catharine and I have one child, age 3. We have been wanting to
HS since he was about 18 months. We plan more children, if it works
out. My husband is very excited about HS and US especially. It
seems to suit our style of parenting and flow nicely from it.

I have a question. Do any of you have any research references on
careers unschooled or HS kids tend to choose when they choose a
profession or vocation? I have heard that it tends to be the more
entrepreneurial type careers, and I wondered if the rest of you had
heard or experienced this as well. I would *love* a website on HS
ressearch, if any of you know one.

Thank you.

catharine
montreal canada

Emily Carr is by portfolio because the artists work is their proof of
talent for entry.
The girl who went to UBC I believe only sat through an interview, I'm
not really sure.
The boy who is in Okanagan University College for pharmacy tech got in
using his parents own transcripts of their homeschooling endeavours. I
believe he only had to do the standard entry for college test.

One of the best ways I've heard of is to find out when SATs or ACTs are
being held in your area and sit them. Those scores as I understand it,
over ride any highschool diploma for most universities and colleges.
