Some things I want to learn/do:

be a lactation consultant
to speak Spanish fluently (I learned in high school, but I've forgotten most of it)
to create a website
to write for publication
to be a better parent to my children
design/draft our own home someday
sew actual clothes -- not just mending and slings (first I need a machine, I guess)
grow herbs -- maybe for next year's garden

I'm sure more things will cross my path, and I'm more confident every day that I'll be able to learn them!

I left school, always an A student, with no desire to be taught anything else. I just wanted to be left alone and I was so tired of being told how smart I was, like that was some obligation to spend my life doing what they wanted me to do to make them proud. Maybe that explains the next 5 yrs spent in non-office jobs and a bad marriage! I was determined to be a non-achiever.

Thanks for the question, Sandra! It's been fun to explore how much of real life is learned away from a building and enforced structure.

Syl

9 Jun 2004, at 6:32am
> What a GREAT thread this is! Thank you Sandra for starting it.

I sit and think about all the things I've learned and then think about
all the things I keep telling myself that I want to learn or do, and
realize that I'm stuck in the old rut of NOT doing these things because
of this or that. I find that I get so busy cleaning the house,
cooking, taking care of the kids or husband, getting ready for the new
baby, unpacking from a recent move and whatever else comes along, that
I'm not doing anything fun! How boring I've become. Time to get out
and start doing things for me.

It's fun to see what everyone else wants to do with their life. I'll
have to think on mine for awhile. My husband has always felt that
since we have kids, we shouldn't be doing anything for ourselves
(especially me, since I'm the one who takes care of them). Well, that
is going to have to change. I love unschooling with my kids, because
I get to unschool too!
