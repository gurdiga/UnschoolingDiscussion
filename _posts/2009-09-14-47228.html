Jill's notes:

The topic was: How do schools and mainstream culture discourage
interests and curiosity and fascination? Why would they? Do they
mean to? What happens? Can adults recover from voices say their
interests are stupid? What if they don't? Can they still unschool?

Homeschooling is like rejecting the school, but unschooling is like
rejecting the curriculum. This was said by Matthew Vilter at the
Good Vibrations conference, and Pam Sorooshian relayed it in the
chat, when we were talking about how school at home discourages
interests also.

For the first question of the topic, Sandra said that is was general
disrespect for children.

"Just because my kids like something doesn't mean I have to LOVE it
too, it means I need to find a way to help them do it and explore
it." Jenny Cyphers

"Some families might not discourage anything specifically, but the
atmosphere at their houses is negative and critical." Sandra Dodd
