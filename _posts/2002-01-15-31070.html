January 11
National Post (Canada) : Canadians home-school for different reasons than
Americans
http://www.nationalpost.com/news/national/story.htm
l?f=/stories/20020111/1098932.html
A small Canadian study published in the Canadian Journal of Education
suggests it is no longer possible to categorize homeschooling families
according to ideology. The author fails to understand, however, that American
homeschooling has undergone a similar diversification.

January 10
Bergen Record : Fair Lawn girl has even Bobby Fischer beat
http://www.bergen.com/news/chessyk200201102.htm
At 13, homeschooler Hana Itkis is the youngest player in this week's U.S.
Chess Championships in Seattle. She broke the record set in 1957 by
14-year-old future legend, Bobby Fischer.

Observer-Reporter : Homeschool advocates push for access
http://www.observer-reporter.com/283977233253637.bsp
The father of a homeschooler in PA has filed a lawsuit against the local
district to gain access to participate on the school's soccer team. More also
on the legislation introduced that would give homeschoolers access.

January 9
National Post : Universities consider opening doors to home-schooled pupils
http://www.nationalpost.com/news/national/story.html?f=/stories/20020109/10764

44.html
"A provincial university association has established formal contact with the
parents of home-school students in Ontario, marking the first time the
teenagers have been recognized as potential applicants to Ontario
post-secondary institutions."

January 8
Christian Science Monitor : Virtual charters: public schooling, at home
http://www.csmonitor.com/2002/0108/p14s1-lepr.html
"More than 700 students across Pennsylvania have enrolled in the Pennsylvania
Virtual Charter School (PAVCS), the second-largest of several cyberschools
now operating in the state. It offers kindergarten through second grade, and
plans to expand gradually through high school." A look at virtual charters
and some families who have chosen them, as well as concerns and opposition
that have arisen regarding these charters.
