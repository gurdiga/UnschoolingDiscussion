Mary Ellen,
I have traveled to Canada with my sons. I was advised by my travel
agent to take along a notorized letter from ex. Boy, was I glad I
did. The border agents (both US and Canada) questioned me because I
was a single parent traveling with minors (they said they checked
both males and females). Without the letter they said they would not
have been able to let us into Victoria. Apparently this is to hinder
parental kidnapping. My travel agent said she had not heard of
border agent problems, but that hotels in Mexico will sometimes not
allow registration unless you have some type of proof that you have
custody or absent parent approval to have kids out of the country.
She thought she was just being cautious because my sons use their
dad's name and tickets were issued in both names. She didn't realize
the border agents would be questioning us. I'd get a letter
(notarized) at a minimum -- even if you have passports. Even if you
don't have to use it, you may be thankful you have it with you. And
what about if child needed doctors/hospital? With this notarized
letter there's no question that you have parental 'power' from their
dad.

Jan in Marysville

If you are in CA, you can go to court and have yourself declared sole legal
guardian of your child. The form is intimidating looking but is really self
explanatory. You can get a fee waiver and do this all yourself for the
price of the packet of forms.

When you are declared sole legal guardian, you can have the court stamp as
many copies as you feel you may need. You carry this document with you.

Been there, done that and it worked quite well at both the borders.

Lynda
