Home Education Magazine Featured on CBN News

VIRGINIA BEACH, VA -- May 25, 2001 -- In one of its top stories
today, CBN News reported that Home Education Magazine's domain name
has been purchased by an overseas company trafficking pornography.
CBN News issued the alert that home-ed-press.com no longer belongs to
Home Education Magazine on its news magazine program, The 700 Club,
seen by 1,000,000 viewers daily. The domain name was purchased by
Home Education Magazine in 1996, but Mark and Helen Hegener, the
magazine's publishers, were recently informed that the registration
had lapsed and an overseas company had purchased the name, which it
now uses to link to a pornographic site. Home Education Magazine, a
winner of the Parents' Choice 2000 Recommended Award, has supported
homeschooling families with its publication and resources for 18
years. Sites that originally linked to Home Education Magazine
through home-ed-press.com should now use
<http://www.home-ed-magazine.com>http://www.home-ed-magazine.com Specific
information on how to help in this effort can be found at
<http://www.home-ed-magazine.com/badurl.html>http://www.home-ed-magazine.com/badurl.html.

You can watch the CBN News segment on The Fox Family Channel at 10
a.m., 11 p.m., and 2 a.m., or on The Trinity Broadcasting Network
(TBN) at 3 p.m. Eastern Time or 12 p.m. Pacific
Time. Additional times and stations can be found at:
<>http://www.cbn.com/partner/Article_Display_Page/1,1183,PTID2546|CHID101060|CIID130064,00.html.

Send a certified letter. State that you have repeatedly asked for copies
(list dates if you have them) of the records. If they cannot find a way to
get you a copy of the records within 5 working days, then you would like to
be put on the agenda of the next school board meeting. Send this to the
principal of the school with a cc to the district superintendant.
