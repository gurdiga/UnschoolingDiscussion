When I asked what they wanted for dinner...they said "Cake!" so..guess what is baking?!

Somehow that's a fitting end to the day we've had today.

As a college teacher I use white board markers a lot. Writing up on the
board in front of a class it is impossible not to get some on my arm
and hand. I developed a sensitivity to these markers and it was BAD -
had a terrible rash that spread from my arms to my face and it was
really hard to get rid of. I finally, after weeks, got rid of it, then
we wanted to check and make sure it was the markers, so I just put a
dab on the inside of my arm and got a big red welt there which spread
as a rash along the inside of my arm.

Just so you'll be suspicious of markers IF your children come down with
a mysterious rash.

My kids used markers all over themselves - Roya still does it at 19 (so
don't hold out hope for them growing out of it <G>) - never had a
problem. But you may want to avoid the white board markers - EXPO was
the brand that I was using.
