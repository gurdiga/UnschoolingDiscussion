The living history place you're thinking about is probably Connor Prairie
near Noblesville. I highly recommend it. Their website is:
http://www.connerprairie.org

I also recommend the Indianapolis Children's Museum. That is a must see.

Great state parks which are not far from Indpls include Turkey Run which is
adjacent to Parke County, the covered bridge capital of the world, and
Spring Mill which is south of Bloomington off of State Road 37. The latter
has a museum dedicated to Mitchell, Indiana's famous son, Gus Grissom, and
a very interesting pioneer village with a working water mill.

John
(Who lived in Indpls from 1993-1997)
