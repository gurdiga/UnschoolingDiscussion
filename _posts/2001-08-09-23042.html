blamed on vaccines, since the medical community has every resource under
the sun to blame an injury or death on some other source. It is very
hard to get any doctor/hospital/whatever to blame a vaccine, even if it's
blatently obvious.
I'm not neccessarily anti-vaccine (we do selected vaccines, on a delayed
schedule) but I am very wary of blindly trusting the government to decide
for me when the best time to vaccinate is. It has little to do with when
the vaccine is the most effective, and more to do with how convenient it
is for the medical community to chart it.
By delaying my children's tetnus shot (and ONLY the tetenus, no D or P
with it!) to after a year, they only needed one and not the standard 3
(or is it 4?) doses that most infants get. works for me.
