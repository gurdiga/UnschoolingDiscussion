Some people emailed me privately asking for a list of other unschooling lists so I though before I go I should post here since there was a recent post stating that there weren't a lot of other lists for unschoolers....I hope this can lay that one to rest. Here are some lists that I have found.
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/unschooling
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/thelearningendeavor
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/CCU-List (this is a great Christian list)
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/RUL (can be like the dotcom list but has been pretty tame lately)
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/FUNNews (a newsletter)
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/UN-SCHOOL
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/schools_out
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/Seedling (another newsletter, really good)
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/UnschoolingfromtheHeart (this looks really good, small but growing)
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/UnschoolersCircle (some of the same people from the dotcom list so has potential for same volative discussion)
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/UnDaily Like this one a lot...talks about your "daily" activities
Some of these are smaller groups but if they got more people to join and post they could be really good lists. I hope to see you on some of them. There are more for individual states so you might want to search for your state. Hope that helps. I think I'll post this list to the dotcom list since someone made a comment that there weren't many other unschooling lists.......I would hate for people to be misled into thinking that was the only list out there.
Rachel

on 9/16/01 5:35 AM, mom2kaalande@... wrote:

> and people should go elsewhere have been
> said which is what "maybe this list isn't for you" surely indicates.

And I think that's the way it gets read by some. Perhaps because when we are
in a learning position we are used to having others tell us what to do.

But it is learning by learning to ask questions of yourself.

(And every time the rewording of the quote is reposted, the beginning of it
is left off: *If* you want such-and-such, then maybe this isn't the place
for you. It is like saying, If you want recipes that contain animal
products, then maybe this vegan list isn't for you.)

It means stop and think what *you* want. Stop and listen to what this
particular place has to offer.

We may think we all want the same thing. We may think there is one best way
to find it. (It's what schools are based on.) But we all want something
different, even if we all call it unschooling. Just as we need to learn that
all children learn differently and need different things, adults need
different ways of learning and different atmospheres to learn it in. Some
may want support for whatever they are doing. Some may want a list full of
day in the life stories. Some may want a place that is nothing but positive
messages. *If* one or more of those is so, then this isn't the list for
them.

Joyce
