Professional Bookkeeping and Accounting
http://home.universalclass.com/i/business/accounting/classes/1703.htm

By completing this course, students will be able to:
1.) Identify the purposes of accounting and explain the terminology
used commonly by bookkeepers and accountants.
2.) Explain typical business events and how they affect financial
statements.
3.) Correctly record transactions in a general ledger and various
journals.
4.) Adjust the accounting data at the end of a period for certain
items, including accruals and deferrals, and summarize that data
in financial statements.
5.) Journalize and post closing entries and prepare a post closing
trial balance.
6.) Explain the accounting systems and practices typical of a
retailing business, including the recording of sales, accounts
receivable, purchases, and accounts payable, and procedures used to
compute inventory and cost of goods sold.
7.) Explain the steps used to internally control cash receipts and
cash payments.
8.) Compute and account for wages, salaries, and payroll taxes,
record tax payments, and file the required tax returns and reports.
9.) Estimate and journalize bad debt losses.
10.) Compute and record the depreciation of fixed assets, depletion
of natural resources, and the amortization of intangible assets.

Art Appreciation
http://arthistory.about.com/library/blcourse_artapp.htm?once=true&

It's About Art: Art Appreciation is an introduction to the world of
art and art history. Equally valuable for absolute beginners as well
as seasoned experts, this course offers an opportunity for students
to learn more about looking at, talking about and perhaps even
collecting, art. From determining what makes something a work of
art, and what materials are used to create it, to pronouncing
artists' names and learning about the buying and selling of art,
It's About Art: Art Appreciation will help you to feel confident
about your knowledge of painting, drawing, sculpture and printmaking.


BRL: Braille Through Remote Learning
http://www.brl.org/

Is an online instructional program that provides teachers, parents,
social workers, and current/future braille transcribers with a
series of three integrated online courses in braille and braille
transcribing. By combining electronic technologies, quality
materials, and expert instructors, the program has as its goal the
provision of a complete braille instructional program to all types of
consumers nationwide who have an interest in some or all aspects of
braille codes.

The program consists of three integrated courses:
Introduction to Braille: a 12-session basic course in braille literacy,
designed for beginning students of braille or for those
who need a braille refresher. In addition to "beginner's basics",
this course includes all Grade 2 contractions and rules.
Braille Transcribers: this course focuses on the production of
braille materials, with an emphasis on the brailling of textbooks (using
the new "Formats" braille code). Discussions of computers
in braille production and tactile graphics is also included in
this course. Prerequisite for this course is a solid foundation
in Grade 2 braille.
Specialized Codes: this course provides a basic introduction to
specialized codes, such as Nemeth mathematics braille, music
braille, computer braille, and chemistry braille. Prerequisite
for this course is a solid foundation in Grade 2 braille.
