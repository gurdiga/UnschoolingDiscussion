Our whole family bikes together. Most of the year it's our primary
mode of transport as well, so we get to spend a lot of time together
on bikes. We often take picnics down the bike trail by the river in
the summertime. With the Burley trailer, you can just stuff
everything in the back and go! Doesn't take any time at all. The
thing hauls over a 100 pounds! We can split the kids up and one takes
the oldest on the tandem and the other hauls the youngest in the
Burley (for longer distances), or to travel light we can put the
youngest in the seat and the oldest rides his own bike (for shorter
distances). If it's just me and the kids, I can haul the Burley
behind the tandem - what a train!

The thing I like best about biking is that we get a lot of time to
talk, and we get to interact with the world as it goes by - smelling
the flowers together, so to speak. For us, it's a great way to spend
time together while being active, which we all love and need. For us
it works, but that doesn't mean I think it's the best thing for
everyone, just one of the many ways we get to spend time as a family
and enjoy each other's company.

Blue Skies,
-Robin-

The MS causes muscle weakness and pain so sometimes I have to walk with a stick. So far I've never needed a wheelchair. MS comes and goes and at the moment is in remission apart from numb fingertips. As
for the fibro, some of the symptons are similar but its biggest characteristic is pain. Cycling is my way of taking control of my body and making it do stuff. If I gave in to the pain I'd spend all day
