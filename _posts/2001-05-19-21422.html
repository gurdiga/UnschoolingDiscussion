Hi, just wanted to give a brief introduction. My name is Terri. I'm 34, married to Mike and we have three children--Jordan is 13, Jessica is 11 and Jenna is almost 2. Originally from South Louisiana, we now live in Kansas (hence the name "kscajuns"). This is our first year homeschooling and what a tough one it has been! I quickly discovered that curriculum wasn't for us, so we've been "winging it" ever since. (Don't know who was more burnt out in the first two months--them or me!) I've heard the term unschooling, but it wasn't until I got onto Unschooling.com, that I realized that's exactly what I've been doing since tossing the gradebook out the window. So, here I am.......I'll probably lurk for a little bit before jumping right into the conversation, so as to give me a chance to see the topics and get to know everyone a little bit. Got to say---it's nice to know that I'm not alone!

Terri

Hello, Mary! You, my friend, know then that a true cajun comes from Lafayette, not New Orleans, like the popular misconception!

My husband is from Metairie, but I spent the better part of my life in Lafayette. (1976-1991) My parents still live there and the kids and I are going down in about a week to visit them. Unlike you, though, the only thing I miss about La. is the *food*! Kansas just doesn't know a thing about spicy! Other than that, my husband and I both agree that Kansas is the best. He works for Union Pacific (before that Southern Pacific) and we've moved quite a bit. Eight towns and four states later, we feel like we've finally made it *home*.

And I sure don't miss the humidity down there! People here in Kansas actually complain when the humidity here gets to 40%!! Can you imagine??

Terri
