Hi all -
Thanks, Sharon of the Swamp, for your words.....I feel the same way....."not
doing" is a choice I make specifically and repeatedly.....I also try to make
the conscious choice to "not do for my kids" (when I think, "I should take
them to ..." I try to stop) .....I do not try to 'provide' opportunities,
books, educational stuffs, etc.....In fact, I'm getting rid of my collection
of such materials.....I am trying to respond to dks.....mostly I think
responding/treating them with lovingkindness is the best thing I can do for
them.....well, I don't really know that except that it is healing to me.....

Some people value collecting books/libraries and having shelves of
'materials'....I don't.....I like to read books and then send them away.....I
don't like 'educational stuff' like microscopes and math manipulatives.....if
it becomes appropriate for our family to get something, we try to....NOT bec.
we are trying to teach something but bec. ds wants to use a microscope just
like he'd like more characters for his 'doll collection' or more Pokemon
cards....

I DO think that NOT DOING is a BIG part of unschooling.......and can be the
most 'educational' part..... : )

Just a thought or two.....
Jane
