Hi,

Sounds interesting. I am a 57 year old full time dad. We have two
older daughters, 10 and 14, who are in public school, and a 7 year old
son that I home school. We adopted the girls from Russia two years ago,
and I home schooled them for the first year. Now, they are in public
school, but I still home school my son, Taylor.

We always had the idea of home schooling Taylor, because he seems
especially bright. The homeschooling is paying off very well, as he
functions way above grade level in all subjects. He loves it, and I
love it. We work hard and play hard every day.

We would be happy to participate,

Chad Holland

> Dear Friends,
>
> My name is Rhonda Barfield and I'm an author (some of you may have heard of
> my books,15-Minute Cooking and Eat Healthy for $50 a Week) and homeschooling
> mother of four. I need your help.
>
> I'm writing a book on lifestyles of homeschooling families, and I'm looking
> for a wide variety of parents who are of all races, religions, and homeschool
> philosophies, as well as from many different regions of the U.S. I am
> purposely avoiding well-known homeschoolers, preferring to write about
> everyday people. High on my priority list are homeschoolers who are Hispanic
> and/or immigrants, fathers who homeschool, observant Jews, people of other
> religions, people who live in a large city like New York, and unschoolers, as
> I have plenty of contacts for WASP families. Would some of you be interested
> in being included as one of the <<stories>> in my book?
>
> Your part would involve answering about 50 questions, either by phone or
> e-mail, about how homeschool works in your family. I will take the answers
> and write them up as a story. Then you get a rough draft, to make sure of
> accuracy and your approval or suggestions for change. I finalize, send you a
> second draft, you approve and that's that. I would include the first and last
> names of each member of your family, but no city, only a state. You may be
> asked to provide some photos.
>
> The book will be published by Kensington Publishing in New York and
> distributed nationally. I'm negotiating with my publisher so that every
> participating family will receive at least a couple of free copies of the
> book, tentatively called <<Real-Life Homeschooling: The Stories of 25
> Families Who Teach Their Children at Home.>>
>
> Please let me know if any of you are interested in being part of this book.
> Thank you in advance!
>
> Rhonda Barfield
