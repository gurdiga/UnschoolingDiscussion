>>>>If you worry about your children, make an effort to be around other
unschooled kids, older than yours, and see if you think they're growing
up okay.

>>
Where does one find these? I should mention that the vast majority of my
city is made up of those "fundamentalist Christian Homeschoolers" ya'll
have been back and forth about. In fact, I've never even met a
homeschooler in my city that didn't homeschool for religious reasons and
our local organization (and fair cooridinators) are all of the same
mindset. I'm so glad that I had the computer! If it weren't for a bunch
of great women who couldn't see that I was wearing a denim skirt and
spoke to my mind/words instead, I'd probably be still desperately trying
to get out of my box. I'm not practiced in knowing how to find real life
women outside the box..... where to start?<<<

Start you own on-line unschoolers group. I did that (and it turned into an
international conference! <G> I had 350 of my closest virtual unschooling
buddies descend on me one weekend! <G>).

I talked a couple of NC unschoolers into doing it---and within a WEEK, they
had 49 unschoolers on their list! The Greenville unschoolers did it----and
hosted a picnic in the mountains this past Tuesday----FIFTEEN unschooling
families showed up---SOOOO COOL!

Post the info about your new e-list on all state/city-wide e-lists. Put up
flyers at the local food co-op, natural food store,libraries---places YOU hang
out and places where unschoolers might hang out.

If you plan it, they will come!

~Kelly

>>>>make an effort to be around other unschooled kids, older than yours,
>>
Where does one find these?>>>

I met one unschooler in my town group where I just moved to. She had been an unschooler in the beginning but as her kids got older she began assimilating into the school-at-home group and became more eclectic. I knew she was an unschooler-at-heart by the way she talked so I asked her about it. She never goes on the internet so she had no other unschoolers near her so she's glad I am in the group now.

I met a second unschooler in the video store here. I saw her get out of a van that was painted like tie dye so that peaked my curiosity about her. I saw her looking at movies with her probably 8 or 9 year old daughter wrapped around her waist. I knew for sure then that she was one of us. I approached her and asked her if she was an unschooler. She was (of course). Unfortunately she was in the process of moving to another state because it would have been cool to get to know her. BTW, it was her kids who painted her van. She bought a whole bunch of spray paint and let them do it.

Crystal
