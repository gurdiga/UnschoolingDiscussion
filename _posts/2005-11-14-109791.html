We just watched Princess Bride on a really big screen yesterday. Our
Homeschool group put on a production of Midsummer Night's Dream. The cast
was composed of over 40 kids ages 5-17...in between shows we needed
something to keep everyone somewhat calm and contained and entertained. The
church we did the show in has a big screen with a set-up for movies. What
was really funny was over half of the kids were reciting the movie word for
word during the whole thing. Great fun!!

My partner and I are not married (well, not really--only in the eyes of
the state <smile>), but we talk about it sometimes. I always say,
whatever vows we come up with have to include him hissing, "Skip to the
end! Skip to the end!" I would love to really do it.

I used to have such severe environmental sensitivities that I would
sometimes wear a mask at big group gatherings like conferences, to
protect myself from perfume and scented grooming products. When I got
tired of explaining why I was wearing it, if someone asked I would say,
"It's just that they're so terribly comfortable. I think everyone will
be wearing them in the future." If the person got the joke, I knew i
had a new friend.

The Princess Bride--is there anything it can't do?

Su
