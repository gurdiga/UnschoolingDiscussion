I agree with BOnnie, and someone else that mentioned their support of this idea.It really has been turned around. I was always taught that it meant everyone helps everyone else, that is in their village or community.......not that it meant that the parents are out working too hard to know their own children, and the village or community(i.e. schools) took care of them for them.  My philosophy on this topic is that we build our own villages of family  & friends, and they are in fact also teachers of  us & our children..as we ,adults & children , alike, also are teachers of them & theirs.and that we are all learning everyday from the people we trust & care about.  SO this would be a "it takes a village to raise a child"    mentality, that I am proud of as these people I have chosen to be in our lives are there for a reason.  They model our values, and they care for us.
