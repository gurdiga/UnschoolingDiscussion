Listening to an interview of David McCullough "1776" on the Diane Rehm show, I caught a bit of his telling that he uses a typewriter to write his books. He said that he "doesn't know how" to type, so does it slowly, but his typing speed is compatible with his thinking while he is writing. He also said that he fears if he used a computer that he would push the wrong button and delete stuff by accident.

This ties into the idea that there are certain things people "have to learn" in order to be successful. McCullough is a very successful author without mastering the computer or learning "correct" keyboarding.

Mary Ellen
