Hmmm. Interesting.

I guess I think that if my life, and my son's life is happy (happy in a
healthy way), that we WILL be morally upright, kind, gentle, respectful,
humble, giving, patient, and self-controlled.

I know that if I'm feeling happy and at peace about how my life is going
along, I'm more likely to do the things I'm not so fond of doing. Like,
take out the recycling and trash, scrub the bathroom, etc. I can do things
that give of myself, and I'm patient and self-controlled.

If I'm not happy with life; when I've struggled with depression, grief, or
whatever, I find it hard to do even the basic things I *do* enjoy, let alone
those things I don't enjoy. I'm selfish, rude, and lazy.

So my goal is that my son grow without the wierd emotional baggage so many
people seem to have that makes them want more STUFF instead of fullfillment,
to do destructive things to be noticed, etc. In other words, I hope that
he'll be happy.
