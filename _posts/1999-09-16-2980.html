Denver By Mrs. Alphabet

Use this poem to identify spot D names and to elicit new D names. Write out
on sentence strips, place in a pocket chart, and then brainstorm other
places to live that begin with B from your class.

David, Dot, and Dino live in Denver with me
Dina, Dusty, and Dominic want to visit and see.
What other D kids do you think live there,
In delightful Denver far away from here?

Interview 6 people and ask them this question: Can you dive? Record the
results.

Now isn't this ducky: http://www.alaska.net/~cccandc/ducks.htm

Take an imaginary trip on a donkey. Write or have children dictate a story
and illustrate. Display children's work in a book form.

Visit Delaware; locate on your map or globe, then go to:
http://www.state.de.us/

Use dots as counters or for simple math problems: estimating, counting,
graphing, adding, sorting, patterns, subtracting, or bingo markers.

Ask the children to bring in dominoes to compare, contrast, and collect.

Dice Math can incorporate counting, graphing, sorting, measuring,
predicting, and comparing.

Use different size stuffed dogs for a math lesson demonstrating: color,
shape, size, sorting, and counting.

Graph how many children were born in Delaware.

Graph how many children have dogs.

Graph how many children take dance lessons .

Develop a list of Disney characters and then go here:
http://disney.go.com/home/homepage/index.html? and please be sure to visit
my friend Cathy Disney's site at: http://members.tripod.com/~luvdisney/

Discuss a dictionary and then go here to display an online dictionary:
http://www.enchantedlearning.com/cgi-bin/F2.cgi/

Use a telephone book to find D names.

If I were a doctor I would _________________, but I wouldn't
__________________.

If I were a dentist I would _____________________, but I wouldn't
__________________.

If I were dirty I would _____________________, but I wouldn't
__________________. Then make this delicious treat:
http://www.ichef.com/ichef-recipes/Cakes/3515.html

Create a predictable chart. "I like ____________ donuts." Another
predictable chart is "My favorite day is ______________________."
Play and teach the children the lyrics to this song:
http://www.fiftiesweb.com/lyrics/dayo.htm

Create class d charts of words, and place child's name at the end of his/her
sentence.

Find objects that are dark in color.

Don't miss out on this darling game:
http://www.billybear4kids.com/games/stickers/dolhouse/stickers.htm

Ask the children to analyze what they think is used to make a Dove bar by
tasting and observing the contents. Then read off and list the ingredients.
Check off the items that the children guessed. Remember check for milk
allergies.

List names that begin with D and talk about what you see or hear.

Check names in the class for D sounds. Make a graph of children that have
the D sound in their name.

Read a poem and have children color code all the d words.
