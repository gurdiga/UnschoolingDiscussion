Lynda sees the problem, IMHO. No matter what one says "the system" --
school, economic, business, media or what not -- will twist it to mean
something else. Many have thaken the wore "community" in our book "Creating
Learning Communities" to mean the local government. Our concept, carefully
explained, was that any group of families who get together to do something
is a community. Homeschool support groups are communities, learning co-ops
re communities, cooperative community life-long learning centers (CCL-LLCs)
are communities. Our thesis is that it easier and more efficient, if not
necessary, for each of us to rely on and help others. That is "community,"
"neighborhood," "village" "extended family" as we see it.

And as Lynda says (and a chapter by a Native American in the book says) that
is the way all peoples have learned until the EuroAmericans changed it all.

Collaborative Unschooling is the name of the game. I'm pleased to see so
much of it on this listserv.
