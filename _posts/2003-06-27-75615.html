It's that time of year when aquaintances ask me if we continue homeschooling in the summer.

I try to explain that we don't homeschool differently in the summer, because the way we learn doesn't look like school anyway. I'm getting tired of trying to explain, and thought that I would just say, "we're taking the summer off", the next time I'm asked.

While pulling weeds this evening, I thought instead that I could say, "Oh, we make it a point to avoid all learning during the summer".

Now I'm eager for someone to ask!

Mary Ellen

This is GOOD, MaryEllen.

It's happening to me, too, and I haven't known just what to say,
though I think I helped one mom see the value of taking the summer to
just watch her kids. Told her a little bit about how I've watched
Katie (My most Unschooled child) learn so many things without me
teaching her, and said "Just relax. Take the whole summer off, and
watch your kids. Just observe them. They're learning, even when
you're NOT teaching them."

And then she wanted to talk about some very cool curriculum she'd
found at a homeschoolers' conference, and about how they tested their
boy (Katie's age...8...and a slow reader) and found out he has no
peripheral vision (gimme a break) AND how they have lenses they can
fit him with to help with that.

ugh. They have THAT kind of thing, at these homeschooler conferences?
Sheesh. But she wasn't going to get the lenses. Too expensive, but
she WAS so relieved to find out the reason WHY her son was such a
late starter and a slow reader...poor mom. Poor kids. Trapped in a
schoolish mentality, and the stress that comes with it.

Well, sheesh. Didn't mean to get all tangential there. :\

"We make it a point to avoid all learning in the summer." Good.

LOL

The first summer after I removed Kass and Kree from school, my own grandma,
the twins great-grandma, asked the same question. My aunts explained to her
that since my kids don't go to school, it doesn't really matter how we do summer.


My grandma, well, she went off. Saying that the girls are so smart they
deserve summer vacation. I tried to explain that we unschool, so they never really
stop learning. She had a fit and a half. She was stuck in that if all the
other kids in the world get a summer, then her great granddaughters deserved one
too! My aunts did no better and just got frustrated with trying to explain it.
Finally I gave in. "You're right Grandma, I won't make them learn anything for
the summer." She was happy and dropped the argument just after saying, "I may
be old, but I know everyone needs a vacation!"

So the topic was dropped and she felt like she had won. I guess what was
more important than the argument, was that my grandma made it clear she supported
our decision to not do school anymore, she just wanted to make sure my girls
had fun too and didn't work too hard during the summer. Even now she doesn't
get what we do, only that I don't make them do anything.

Rhonda
