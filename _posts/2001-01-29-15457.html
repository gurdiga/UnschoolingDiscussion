My kids haven't discovered the video games. I don't limit computer
games, except that we only have one computer and they need to quit when
it's my turn or time for dinner, etc. Computer usage hasn't been a
problem (yet). They sometimes will play for a long time, but they
usually get tired of it or we need to go somewhere. I'm hoping to have
another computer in a year or two. They are 8 and 5 now.

I do limit watching TV. We don't have cable, which greatly limits TV
appeal! 8 yr old rarely asks to watch a movie, but will watch if one is
going. I'm not really sure why I have this limit, except that too much
TV seems to "dull" them. We did have a TV WEEK after Christmas. 5yr dd
asked if she could check out her limit of movies at the library, so we
each did and came home with 15 movies that we could keep for a week and I
said they could watch whenever they wanted. I'm happy to say that we
ended up not seeing all of them. In fact, she preferred to re-view one
or two rather than see a new one. But we did spend more time plopped on
the couch than normal.

In Mother Dance (by same author as Dance of Anger, Lerner?), she writes
of an experiment with her sons. She designated a shelf in the pantry
where they each had their own space. They went to the store and they
each got to buy their favorite candy. Then mom made sure that the pantry
was always well stocked with their goodies and they had unlimited access.
(Can you imagine? Johnny, your M&Ms are running low. Put it on the
grocery list!) If I recall correctly, they at first overdid the sweets
and then got tired of them. In fact, she tells of her younger son
"sneaking" grapes at a buffet party. The parents were boycotting grapes
because of worker issues, so they never had them in the house. It's not
a homeschooling book, but I enjoyed reading her mothering insights. Got
some good laughs too.
