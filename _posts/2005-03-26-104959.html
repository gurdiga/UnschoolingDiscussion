It's not naturopaths or any one type of health system that is to blame I don't think. I think it's anytime anyone of them tells you to approach something from a position of fear instead of a position of trust. I have seen that naturopaths can cause people to look at the world as a scary, fearful place and make them worried that they can't trust themselves to know what they should eat. I think fear is one of the worst things we can do for our health. So yes, I agree, but I also think that more mainstream doctors are full of fear. It's just that (as a generalization) mainstream doctors are afraid of germs and can't trust the body, and naturopaths are afraid of toxins and can't trust the body. Kind of like schools can't trust the mind to learn.

I've also found that intuitive energy medicine is more in line with what I've learned about natural learning through unschooling. When my homeopath talks about trusting your instincts it's just like hearing an unschooler talk. The philosophies fit very nicely together for me.

Sheila
