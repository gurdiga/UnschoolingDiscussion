>In particular, I'm concerned about
>the state mandated testing.

We just had a discussion about this on a Colorado hs yahoo group that you might like to check out: http://groups.yahoo.com/group/EHCO/ The subject line was "I'm scared".

Testing or evaluations are required every other year starting "third grade".

Options besides testing are evaluations by a "qualified person" - CO licensed teacher, a teacher employed by an independent or parochial school, a licensed psychologist, or a person with a graduate degree in education; or signing up with an umbrella school.

It really is quite easy. The liaison of school district that I happen to be in is incredibly supportive of homeschooling. The licensed psychologist that I used for my dd's evaluation was respectful and very supportive of unschooling. All he did was read over my notes that I had kept for most of the year and wrote a few sentences stating that dd was progressing according to her age.

Among the families in our unschooling group, a few know licensed teachers, who are willing to sign off for our future evaluations. So we won't have to pay the costs of a licensed psychologist.
