Standardized Testing

Testing is required only under Section 59-65-40; therefore, testing is
only required by state law if you homeschool through the school board
of the district you reside in. However, each umbrella association
decides whether testing is or is not required for their members. CHASE
SC does not require testing. If you would like to test your children
even though CHASE SC does not require testing you may contact the
following for information on their testing programs:

1. HSLDA web site: http://www.hslda.org/ email address: info@...
Tests HSLDA members may rent from HSLDA-

The Woodcock-Johnson PsychoEducational Battery-Revised, Tests of
Achievement: This individually administered test is useful for
children who may not test well in a group setting. It must be
administered by a special education teacher.

Brigance Diagnostic Inventories: These are tests that parents or
professionals may administer to determine what skills a child has or
has not mastered. These tests are used routinely in public and private
schools to develop an IEP.

age 7.

We live in Minnesota. This is the first year that my oldest (7.5) has to
test. You do not report the test scores here, you just need to keep them on
file. I would have no problem playing dot-to-dot with the answer key. I
would like to devalue the test.

The problem is my husband. He is still not completely on board with
homeschooling. I'm afraid if some parts of the test come back showing
weaknesses, he is going to stress out. I have tried to print out stuff for
him to read that shows how inaccurate tests are and how they can hurt a
child's self esteem.

She is behind grade level in reading and math. I do not care nor stress
about this. I am only worried my husband will see bad test scores and fight
to put her in school. A compromise might be to do school at home, which is
almost as bad.


So I am thinking of ordering the CAT/5 and basically cheating on it. But in
a way that my daughter wouldn't know. She would stress way to much about
cheating. So I figured I would have her do some of it.

So has anyone else done this to safeguard their choice to homeschool?

No testing arguments:

1. Adults are never assumed to take the same amount of time as each other
to learn things. It takes some adults 2 years to finish college....it takes
some 15 or more. Why isn't that ok for kids too?

2. Standardized tests are basically competitions. No matter how high you
score if others score higher, you look bad. Adults would rarely consider
entering a competition in an area they do not love and feel competent. Yet,
we force that on kids. Some kids enjoy reading, some never do. Some kids
enjoy math, some never do. I'm not saying that well, but hopefully you get
my drift.

Julie S.
